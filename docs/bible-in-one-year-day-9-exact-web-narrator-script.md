# Bible In One Year - Day 9 Exact WEB Narrator Script

## Day 9 - Abraham's Test and Legacy

**Reading:** Genesis 21, 22, 23, and 24  
**Bible text:** World English Bible, Genesis 21-24  
**Goal:** Read the passage clearly, pause briefly between natural sections, and give a basic listener enough context to understand what is happening.

## Estimated Length

**Estimated script word count:** about 5,300 words  
**Estimated spoken time:** about 25-32 minutes, depending on narration pace and pause length

Day 9 is longer than Day 8 because Genesis 24 is a full long story by itself. This keeps every verse, but the narrator explanations are intentionally short.

## Opening

Welcome to Day 9 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Genesis 21, 22, 23, and 24.

This is a major turning point in Abraham's story. Isaac is finally born. Hagar and Ishmael are sent away, but God still sees them and protects them. Abraham faces the hardest test of his life. Sarah dies. And by the end, Isaac receives Rebekah, which moves the promise into the next generation.

As we read, listen for one main theme: God keeps His promise, but the promise still has to be trusted, protected, and passed forward.

## Genesis 21:1-5

**Scripture**

1. Yahweh visited Sarah as he had said, and Yahweh did to Sarah as he had spoken.

2. Sarah conceived, and bore Abraham a son in his old age, at the set time of which God had spoken to him.

3. Abraham called his son who was born to him, whom Sarah bore to him, Isaac.

4. Abraham circumcised his son, Isaac, when he was eight days old, as God had commanded him.

5. Abraham was one hundred years old when his son, Isaac, was born to him.

**Narrator**

After years of waiting, the promise finally arrives. Isaac is born exactly as God said. Genesis wants us to see that God's timing may feel long, but His word does not fail.

## Genesis 21:6-7

**Scripture**

6. Sarah said, "God has made me laugh. Everyone who hears will laugh with me."

7. She said, "Who would have said to Abraham, that Sarah would nurse children? For I have borne him a son in his old age."

**Narrator**

Sarah's laughter changes. Earlier, she laughed in disbelief. Now she laughs with joy. Isaac's name is tied to laughter, because God has turned impossible waiting into celebration.

## Genesis 21:8-11

**Scripture**

8. The child grew, and was weaned. Abraham made a great feast on the day that Isaac was weaned.

9. Sarah saw the son of Hagar the Egyptian, whom she had borne to Abraham, mocking.

10. Therefore she said to Abraham, "Cast out this handmaid and her son! For the son of this handmaid will not be heir with my son, Isaac."

11. The thing was very grievous in Abraham's sight on account of his son.

**Narrator**

The joy of Isaac's birth is followed by family pain. Ishmael is still Abraham's son, and sending him away grieves Abraham deeply. The promise is clear, but the family situation is complicated.

## Genesis 21:12-14

**Scripture**

12. God said to Abraham, "Don't let it be grievous in your sight because of the boy, and because of your handmaid. In all that Sarah says to you, listen to her voice. For from Isaac will your seed be called.

13. I will also make a nation of the son of the handmaid, because he is your seed."

14. Abraham rose up early in the morning, and took bread and a bottle of water, and gave it to Hagar, putting it on her shoulder; and gave her the child, and sent her away. She departed, and wandered in the wilderness of Beersheba.

**Narrator**

God makes two things clear. The covenant line will go through Isaac. But Ishmael is not forgotten. God will also make a nation from him because he is Abraham's son.

## Genesis 21:15-19

**Scripture**

15. The water in the bottle was spent, and she cast the child under one of the shrubs.

16. She went and sat down opposite him, a good way off, about a bow shot away. For she said, "Don't let me see the death of the child." She sat over against him, and lifted up her voice, and wept.

17. God heard the voice of the boy. The angel of God called to Hagar out of the sky, and said to her, "What ails you, Hagar? Don't be afraid. For God has heard the voice of the boy where he is.

18. Get up, lift up the boy, and hold him in your hand. For I will make him a great nation."

19. God opened her eyes, and she saw a well of water. She went, filled the bottle with water, and gave the boy drink.

**Narrator**

This is a mercy moment. Hagar thinks Ishmael is going to die, but God hears him. God opens her eyes to water. The wilderness is real, but God is present there too.

## Genesis 21:20-21

**Scripture**

20. God was with the boy, and he grew. He lived in the wilderness, and became, as he grew up, an archer.

21. He lived in the wilderness of Paran. His mother took a wife for him out of the land of Egypt.

**Narrator**

Ishmael's story continues. He is outside the covenant line of Isaac, but he is not outside God's sight. The text says God was with the boy.

## Genesis 21:22-24

**Scripture**

22. It happened at that time, that Abimelech and Phicol the captain of his army spoke to Abraham, saying, "God is with you in all that you do.

23. Now, therefore, swear to me here by God that you will not deal falsely with me, nor with my son, nor with my son's son. But according to the kindness that I have done to you, you shall do to me, and to the land in which you have lived as a foreigner."

24. Abraham said, "I will swear."

**Narrator**

Abimelech can see that God is with Abraham. Abraham still lives as a foreigner in the land, but God's blessing on him is visible.

## Genesis 21:25-27

**Scripture**

25. Abraham complained to Abimelech because of a water well, which Abimelech's servants had violently taken away.

26. Abimelech said, "I don't know who has done this thing. Neither did you tell me, neither did I hear of it, until today."

27. Abraham took sheep and cattle, and gave them to Abimelech. Those two made a covenant.

**Narrator**

Water matters in this world. A well means survival, stability, and future life. Abraham makes peace, but he also makes sure the well is recognized.

## Genesis 21:28-31

**Scripture**

28. Abraham set seven ewe lambs of the flock by themselves.

29. Abimelech said to Abraham, "What do these seven ewe lambs which you have set by themselves mean?"

30. He said, "You shall take these seven ewe lambs from my hand, that it may be a witness to me, that I have dug this well."

31. Therefore he called that place Beersheba, because they both swore there.

**Narrator**

The seven lambs become a witness. Beersheba becomes connected to oath and covenant. Abraham is still waiting for the full land promise, but he now has a recognized place.

## Genesis 21:32-34

**Scripture**

32. So they made a covenant at Beersheba. Abimelech rose up with Phicol, the captain of his army, and they returned into the land of the Philistines.

33. Abraham planted a tamarisk tree in Beersheba, and called there on the name of Yahweh, the Everlasting God.

34. Abraham lived as a foreigner in the land of the Philistines many days.

**Narrator**

Abraham plants a tree and worships Yahweh, the Everlasting God. The tree is a small sign of staying, trusting, and calling on God while still living as a foreigner.

## Genesis 22:1-5

**Scripture**

1. It happened after these things, that God tested Abraham, and said to him, "Abraham!" He said, "Here I am."

2. He said, "Now take your son, your only son, whom you love, even Isaac, and go into the land of Moriah. Offer him there for a burnt offering on one of the mountains which I will tell you of."

3. Abraham rose early in the morning, and saddled his donkey, and took two of his young men with him, and Isaac his son. He split the wood for the burnt offering, and rose up, and went to the place of which God had told him.

4. On the third day Abraham lifted up his eyes, and saw the place far off.

5. Abraham said to his young men, "Stay here with the donkey. The boy and I will go yonder. We will worship, and come back to you."

**Narrator**

This is the hardest test in Abraham's life. Isaac is the promised son. So this test is not only emotional; it seems to touch the promise itself. Abraham obeys, but the tension is heavy.

## Genesis 22:6-8

**Scripture**

6. Abraham took the wood of the burnt offering and laid it on Isaac his son. He took in his hand the fire and the knife. They both went together.

7. Isaac spoke to Abraham his father, and said, "My father?" He said, "Here I am, my son." He said, "Here is the fire and the wood, but where is the lamb for a burnt offering?"

8. Abraham said, "God will provide himself the lamb for a burnt offering, my son." So they both went together.

**Narrator**

Isaac notices what is missing. There is wood, fire, and a knife, but no lamb. Abraham's answer becomes one of the central lines of the chapter: God will provide.

## Genesis 22:9-12

**Scripture**

9. They came to the place which God had told him of. Abraham built the altar there, and laid the wood in order, bound Isaac his son, and laid him on the altar, on the wood.

10. Abraham stretched forth his hand, and took the knife to kill his son.

11. The angel of Yahweh called to him out of the sky, and said, "Abraham, Abraham!" He said, "Here I am."

12. He said, "Don't lay your hand on the boy, neither do anything to him. For now I know that you fear God, seeing you have not withheld your son, your only son, from me."

**Narrator**

God stops Abraham. Isaac is not taken. The test reveals Abraham's trust, but God does not allow the promised son to die on that altar.

## Genesis 22:13-14

**Scripture**

13. Abraham lifted up his eyes, and looked, and saw that behind him was a ram caught in the thicket by his horns. Abraham went and took the ram, and offered him up for a burnt offering instead of his son.

14. Abraham called the name of that place Yahweh Will Provide. As it is said to this day, "On Yahweh's mountain, it will be provided."

**Narrator**

The ram is offered instead of Isaac. Abraham names the place Yahweh Will Provide. The point is not that Abraham loses his son. The point is that God provides.

## Genesis 22:15-19

**Scripture**

15. The angel of Yahweh called to Abraham a second time out of the sky,

16. and said, "I have sworn by myself, says Yahweh, because you have done this thing, and have not withheld your son, your only son,

17. that I will bless you greatly, and I will multiply your seed greatly like the stars of the heavens, and like the sand which is on the seashore. Your seed will possess the gate of his enemies.

18. In your seed will all the nations of the earth be blessed, because you have obeyed my voice."

19. So Abraham returned to his young men, and they rose up and went together to Beersheba. Abraham lived at Beersheba.

**Narrator**

The promise is repeated again: descendants, blessing, victory, and blessing for all nations. Abraham's obedience does not create the promise, but it shows his trust in the God who gave it.

## Genesis 22:20-24

**Scripture**

20. It happened after these things, that it was told Abraham, saying, "Behold, Milcah, she also has borne children to your brother Nahor:

21. Uz his firstborn, Buz his brother, Kemuel the father of Aram,

23. Bethuel became the father of Rebekah. These eight Milcah bore to Nahor, Abraham's brother.

24. His concubine, whose name was Reumah, also bore Tebah, Gaham, Tahash, and Maacah.

**Narrator**

This family list may feel small, but it sets up the next major story. Rebekah is introduced here before Genesis 24 brings her into Isaac's life.

## Genesis 23:1-4

**Scripture**

**Narrator**

Sarah dies in the land of Canaan. Abraham mourns her, and he also needs a burial place. He is still a foreigner, but he is about to buy his first piece of the promised land.

**Scripture**

Abraham is respected, but he wants the land legally and clearly. He asks to pay full price. This burial place will become part of the family's future.

**Scripture**

13. He spoke to Ephron in the audience of the people of the land, saying, "But if you will, please hear me. I will give the price of the field. Take it from me, and I will bury my dead there."

The conversation happens publicly at the city gate. Abraham keeps insisting on paying. He wants no confusion later about ownership.

**Scripture**

**Narrator**

Abraham pays the price. Even in grief, he acts with honor and clarity. The promise of land is still future, but this is a real beginning.

Sarah is buried in Canaan. Abraham owns a burial place there. It is a sad chapter, but it also anchors the family story in the land God promised.

## Personal Outro

That is Day 9: Abraham's Test and Legacy.

Genesis 21 begins with the promise finally arriving. Isaac is born, and Sarah laughs with joy. But the chapter also reminds us that joy and pain can sit close together. Hagar and Ishmael are sent away, and yet God sees them, hears them, and provides water in the wilderness.

Genesis 22 brings Abraham's hardest test. Isaac is the son Abraham loves, and he is the son of the promise. But at the mountain, Abraham learns the line we need to remember: Yahweh will provide.

Genesis 23 slows down for grief. Sarah dies, and Abraham mourns. But even in that grief, he buys a burial place in Canaan. The promise of land is still not fully possessed, but a first piece is held.

Genesis 24 shows the promise moving forward. Abraham sends his servant. The servant prays. Rebekah appears. God leads the whole journey. And Isaac is comforted after his mother's death.

The real-life part is this: sometimes God's promise does not remove hard moments. Isaac is born, but Hagar still weeps. Abraham obeys, but the mountain still feels terrifying. Sarah is part of the promise, but Abraham still has to bury her. Isaac is chosen, but he still needs comfort.

Faith is not pretending life is easy. Faith is learning that God can keep leading through birth, wilderness, testing, grief, and new beginnings.

God provides on the mountain. God provides water in the wilderness. God provides a future for Isaac. And He keeps the promise moving forward.

That leads us into Day 10, where the story moves deeper into Isaac's family and the beginning of Jacob and Esau.
