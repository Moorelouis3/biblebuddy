# Day 99 Narrator Script

## Opening

Welcome to Day 99 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading First Chronicles 21 through 24.

The title for today is The Temple Site and Priestly Order.

This day begins with David's census and the plague that follows.

But out of that judgment, the temple site is identified.

David buys Ornan's threshingfloor and offers sacrifices.

Then David prepares Solomon for temple building and organizes Levites and priests for service.

Day 99 teaches that God can turn a place of judgment into a place prepared for worship.

## First Chronicles 21

First Chronicles 21 begins with Satan standing up against Israel and provoking David to number Israel.

Joab resists the command, but David's word prevails.

The numbering displeases God, and He smites Israel.

David confesses that he has sinned greatly.

Through Gad, God gives David three choices of judgment.

David chooses to fall into the hand of the LORD because His mercies are very great.

The plague comes, and the angel reaches Jerusalem.

The LORD says, It is enough.

David sees the angel by the threshingfloor of Ornan the Jebusite.

Gad tells David to build an altar there.

Ornan offers the place freely, but David refuses to take for the LORD what costs him nothing.

He buys the place, offers burnt offerings and peace offerings, and the LORD answers by fire from heaven.

This place becomes central.

Judgment stops, sacrifice is offered, and the future temple site comes into view.

## First Chronicles 22

First Chronicles 22 begins with David saying, This is the house of the LORD God, and this is the altar of burnt offering for Israel.

David gathers materials for the temple.

He prepares stone, iron, brass, cedar, gold, and silver in abundance.

Solomon is young and tender, and the house must be exceeding magnifical.

David explains to Solomon that he wanted to build, but the word of the LORD said he had shed much blood and made great wars.

Solomon, a man of rest, will build the house.

David charges him to be strong, keep the law of the LORD, and build.

Then David commands the princes of Israel to help Solomon.

This chapter is about preparation.

David cannot build the temple, but he can prepare for the one who will.

## First Chronicles 23

First Chronicles 23 shows David old and full of days, making Solomon king over Israel.

Then he gathers the Levites.

The Levites are numbered and assigned for work.

Some are set forward for the work of the house of the LORD.

Some are officers and judges.

Some are porters.

Some praise the LORD with instruments.

The chapter also explains that because the LORD has given rest to His people and will dwell in Jerusalem forever, the Levites no longer need to carry the tabernacle and vessels as before.

Their service is being ordered around the temple.

This is a transition from wilderness movement to settled worship.

The Levites are to stand every morning and evening to thank and praise the LORD.

## First Chronicles 24

First Chronicles 24 gives the divisions of the sons of Aaron.

The priestly service is organized by lots.

Eleazar's sons and Ithamar's sons are ordered for service.

The chapter lists courses of priests and Levites.

For modern readers, the names can feel distant, but the meaning is practical.

Worship requires order.

The priests do not simply improvise holy service.

They receive assigned courses before the LORD.

The chapter ends with lots cast over against their brethren, showing that the service is arranged fairly and reverently.

## Full Lesson Review

Day 99 moves from sin to sacrifice to preparation.

David's census brings judgment.

The plague stops at Ornan's threshingfloor.

David buys the site and worships.

Then he prepares Solomon, gathers materials, and organizes Levites and priests.

The day teaches that worship is costly, ordered, and rooted in mercy after judgment.

## Slow Walk

The most important movement is the transformation of the threshingfloor.

It begins as a place where judgment is stopped.

It becomes the place David identifies as the house of the LORD.

This is grace.

The future temple site is connected to mercy, sacrifice, and God's answer by fire.

Also notice David's humility.

He cannot do everything.

He cannot build the temple himself.

But he can prepare materials, instruct Solomon, and organize the servants.

Faithfulness sometimes means preparing what another generation will complete.

## Deeper Teaching

Day 99 begins in sin but moves toward worship.

David's census is dangerous because it measures strength in a way that forgets dependence.

Joab sees the danger, but David insists.

When judgment comes, David confesses.

That confession matters.

He does not call the sin a small administrative problem.

He says he has sinned greatly.

Then David chooses to fall into the hand of the LORD because God's mercies are very great.

The plague is severe, but mercy interrupts it.

The angel stands by Ornan's threshingfloor, and the LORD says it is enough.

That place becomes the hinge of the day.

David sees judgment.

David builds an altar.

David refuses cheap worship.

David buys the site.

The LORD answers by fire.

Then David says this is the house of the LORD God.

The future temple site is born out of stopped judgment and accepted sacrifice.

First Chronicles 22 then shows David preparing with intensity.

Solomon is young.

The work is great.

The house is for the LORD.

David gathers materials in abundance.

He also explains why he cannot build.

His wars and bloodshed mean Solomon, the man of rest, will build.

David's role is not useless because he cannot finish the project.

He can prepare.

He can charge.

He can gather.

He can instruct.

He can strengthen Solomon.

First Chronicles 23 and 24 show worship becoming organized.

The Levites are counted and assigned.

Some serve in the house.

Some judge.

Some guard.

Some praise.

The priests are divided into courses.

The work is ordered because worship matters.

The people of God are not left to improvisation when the house of the LORD is being prepared.

This is a major Chronicles theme.

The temple is not only a building.

It is a whole ordered life of service around God's presence.

## Extended Narration

Day 99 begins with a dangerous moment in David's life.

David numbers Israel.

Counting people is not always sinful in the Bible, but this numbering is presented as wrong because it comes from a heart moving in pride, control, or misplaced security.

David is tempted to measure the strength of the kingdom in numbers.

Chronicles wants the reader to see how quickly even a faithful king can turn from trust to self-reliance.

Joab senses the danger.

He asks why David would require this thing.

But the king's word prevails, and the census goes forward.

Then God is displeased.

David confesses, I have sinned greatly.

That confession matters.

David does not blame Joab.

He does not blame the people.

He does not explain it away as leadership strategy.

He calls it sin.

The judgment that follows is severe, and the angel of the LORD comes against Jerusalem.

Then comes one of the most beautiful mercy phrases in the reading.

The LORD says, It is enough.

That means judgment is stopped by God's mercy, not because the sin was small.

The place of judgment becomes the place where David is told to build an altar.

Ornan's threshingfloor becomes holy ground in the story.

David insists on paying the full price because he will not offer burnt offerings without cost.

That line teaches something important about worship.

True worship is not built on giving God what costs us nothing.

David buys the place.

He offers sacrifices.

The LORD answers by fire.

The angel puts up his sword.

Mercy meets sacrifice at the place that will become tied to the temple.

That is why this reading is so important.

The temple site is connected to judgment halted by mercy.

It is connected to confession.

It is connected to sacrifice.

It is connected to the LORD answering.

Then chapter 22 turns toward preparation.

David gathers materials for the temple even though Solomon will build it.

That is a mature kind of service.

David does not say, If I cannot build it, I will not help.

He prepares with strength for work another person will complete.

He charges Solomon to be strong, to build, and to keep the law of the LORD.

This is not only construction advice.

It is spiritual leadership.

David knows the house of God must be built by obedience, not merely by stone, timber, and gold.

Chapters 23 and 24 then organize the Levites and priests.

Modern readers may be tempted to skip the divisions and courses, but Chronicles is showing that worship has order.

There are people appointed to serve.

There are duties assigned.

There is structure around the temple so that the life of worship does not depend on improvisation.

The Levites are numbered for service.

The priests are arranged by their houses.

The whole scene says that God's presence among His people shapes daily life, work, leadership, and responsibility.

For a beginner, Day 99 teaches that God can turn a place of failure into a place of worship.

David sins by numbering the people.

God judges.

David confesses.

God shows mercy.

Sacrifice is offered.

The temple future begins to come into focus.

Then the reading teaches that worship is not only a private feeling.

It requires prepared hearts.

It requires ordered service.

It requires leaders who understand that God's house belongs to God.

The same king who failed in pride now prepares the next generation to build.

That is mercy with responsibility.

## Final Guided Recap

Day 99 teaches that mercy can transform a place.

Ornan's threshingfloor could have been remembered only as a place of terror.

Instead, it becomes the place where sacrifice is offered and the temple site is identified.

The day also teaches that preparation is holy.

David cannot build the temple, but he prepares with faith.

He gathers materials.

He charges Solomon.

He orders Levites.

He arranges priests.

He sets the next generation up to obey.

That is not failure.

That is faithful stewardship.

The listener should leave this day seeing costly worship, generational preparation, and ordered service as part of one story.

## Chapter Listening Guide

In chapter 21, listen for mercy interrupting judgment.

David sins.

The plague comes.

The angel reaches Jerusalem.

Then the LORD says, It is enough.

That phrase changes the future.

The altar at Ornan's threshingfloor becomes a place where sacrifice and mercy meet.

In chapter 22, listen for preparation with humility.

David cannot build the temple.

But he does not become passive.

He prepares materials in abundance.

He encourages Solomon.

He calls the leaders to help.

He turns limitation into faithful preparation.

In chapter 23, listen for Levites in a new season.

The tabernacle no longer needs to be carried the same way.

The LORD has given rest.

Service now centers on the house that will be built.

Morning and evening praise become part of ordered worship.

In chapter 24, listen for assigned priestly service.

The lots, divisions, and names show that worship is not casual.

The priests serve by order before the LORD.

Holy work is shared, structured, and remembered.

## Closing

As you finish Day 99, remember David's words: I will not take that which is thine for the LORD, nor offer burnt offerings without cost.

Worship that honors God is not careless or cheap.
