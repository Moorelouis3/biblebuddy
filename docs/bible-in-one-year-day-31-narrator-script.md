# Bible In One Year - Day 31 Narrator Script

## Day 31 - The Tabernacle Is Finished

**Reading:** Exodus 37 through 40
**Bible text:** King James Version, Exodus 37-40
**Audio style:** Narrator teaching with Scripture reading

## Opening

Welcome to Day 31 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Exodus 37 through 40.

This is the end of the book of Exodus.

And it is not a throwaway ending.

Exodus began with Israel groaning under Pharaoh.

Their bodies were enslaved.

Their sons were threatened.

Their future looked trapped under the power of Egypt.

But now the book ends with a completely different picture.

Not Pharaoh in the center.

Not bricks in the center.

Not fear in the center.

The glory of the LORD fills the tabernacle in the middle of the camp.

That is the movement of Exodus.

From slavery to worship.

From bondage to presence.

From Pharaoh's rule to God's glory.

These chapters can feel repetitive because many of the tabernacle details were already given earlier.

But the repetition is the point.

Earlier, God gave the pattern.

Now Israel obeys the pattern.

That matters deeply after the golden calf.

In Exodus 32, the people said, let us make.

They made worship out of fear, impatience, and imagination.

But here, the repeated heartbeat is different.

As the LORD commanded Moses.

As the LORD commanded Moses.

As the LORD commanded Moses.

The people who once gave gold for an idol now bring gold, silver, bronze, fabric, skill, and labor for God's dwelling.

So today we will see the ark made.

We will see the mercy seat made.

We will see the table, lampstand, incense altar, bronze altar, laver, courtyard, and priestly garments completed.

We will see Moses inspect the work.

We will see the tabernacle raised.

And then we will see the moment Exodus has been moving toward:

The cloud covers the tent.

The glory fills the tabernacle.

And God dwells among the people He rescued.

## Exodus 37:1-9

**Scripture**

And Bezaleel made the ark of shittim wood.

And he overlaid it with pure gold within and without.

And he made the mercy seat of pure gold.

And he made two cherubims of gold.

And the cherubims spread out their wings on high, and covered with their wings over the mercy seat.

**Narrator**

Exodus 37 begins with the ark.

That is important because the ark belongs at the center of the Most Holy Place.

It is the holy chest connected with God's covenant testimony.

The mercy seat sits above it.

And the cherubim overshadow the mercy seat.

So the center of Israel's worship is not Israel's achievement.

It is God's word and God's mercy.

That matters after the golden calf.

Israel cannot simply move on as if sin was small.

Mercy has to stand at the center because sin needs covering.

The ark reminds us that life with God is covenant life.

The mercy seat reminds us that covenant life depends on God's mercy.

And the cherubim remind us that this mercy is holy mercy.

God is near, but He is never casual.

## Exodus 37:10-16

**Scripture**

And he made the table of shittim wood.

And he overlaid it with pure gold.

And he made the vessels which were upon the table, his dishes, and his spoons, and his bowls.

**Narrator**

Next Bezalel makes the table.

This is the table connected with the bread of the Presence.

That may sound like a small furniture detail, but it teaches something warm and steady.

God's dwelling includes provision and fellowship.

Bread belongs in the holy place.

The people live because God provides.

And the table stands as a continual sign that Israel's life is before the LORD.

Even the dishes, spoons, and bowls are made carefully.

Those details are not filler.

They show that holy worship includes ordinary service pieces too.

In God's house, the small things matter because they serve the larger purpose.

## Exodus 37:17-24

**Scripture**

And he made the candlestick of pure gold.

Of beaten work made he the candlestick.

His shaft, and his branch, his bowls, his knops, and his flowers, were of the same.

And he made his seven lamps.

**Narrator**

Now the lampstand is made.

The KJV calls it the candlestick, but this is the golden lampstand for the holy place.

It brings light into the sanctuary.

And it is beautiful.

Branches.

Bowls like almonds.

Knops.

Flowers.

Seven lamps.

This is not bare utility.

God's worship space includes beauty, light, order, and life-shaped design.

The lampstand reminds Israel that holy service happens in God-given light.

The priests do not serve in darkness.

God provides light for the place where His worship is carried out.

## Exodus 37:25-29

**Scripture**

And he made the incense altar of shittim wood.

And he overlaid it with pure gold.

And he made the holy anointing oil, and the pure incense of sweet spices.

**Narrator**

The incense altar is also made.

This altar stands near the veil.

It is connected with fragrant incense rising before the LORD.

That matters because Exodus is teaching that nearness to God is ordered and holy.

Even smell is brought into worship.

Even fragrance belongs to God.

The anointing oil marks people and objects as set apart.

The incense is not ordinary perfume for ordinary use.

It belongs to the LORD.

So Exodus 37 slows us down inside the holy place.

Ark.

Mercy seat.

Table.

Lampstand.

Incense altar.

The furniture is preaching.

God is present.

God provides.

God gives light.

God receives holy worship.

And God meets His people through mercy.

## Exodus 38:1-8

**Scripture**

And he made the altar of burnt offering of shittim wood.

And he made all the vessels of the altar.

And he made the laver of brass, and the foot of it of brass, of the lookingglasses of the women assembling.

**Narrator**

Exodus 38 moves outside into the courtyard.

The bronze altar is made for sacrifice.

That is the first major lesson of the courtyard.

Approach to God requires atonement.

No one strolls into God's presence as if sin is light.

The altar stands before the holy place because sacrifice comes before approach.

Then the laver is made.

The laver is for washing.

The priests will wash their hands and feet before serving.

So the altar teaches sacrifice.

The laver teaches cleansing.

And there is a beautiful detail here.

The bronze for the laver comes from the mirrors of the women who served at the entrance.

Objects once used for reflection become part of priestly cleansing.

Personal gifts are transformed into holy service.

## Exodus 38:9-20

**Scripture**

And he made the court.

The hangings of the court were of fine twined linen.

And the gate of the court was an hanging of blue, and purple, and scarlet.

All the pins of the tabernacle, and of the court round about, were of brass.

**Narrator**

The courtyard is built around the tabernacle.

This creates a defined sacred space.

That matters.

God provides access, but not random access.

There is a way in.

There is an entrance.

There are boundaries.

There are curtains, posts, sockets, hooks, pins, and measurements.

That may feel like a lot of detail.

But the message is simple:

God is near, and God is holy.

Both are true.

The courtyard says, You may come.

But it also says, Come the way God gives.

Grace and holiness belong together.

## Exodus 38:21-31

**Scripture**

This is the sum of the tabernacle.

All the gold that was occupied for the work was gold of the offering.

And the silver of them that were numbered of the congregation was an hundred talents.

And the brass of the offering was seventy talents.

**Narrator**

Then Exodus records the materials.

Gold.

Silver.

Bronze.

Amounts.

Uses.

Oversight.

This may sound like bookkeeping.

But it matters.

The people gave willingly, and those gifts were handled with accountability.

Worshipful generosity should not be sloppy.

Holy work should not be hidden in confusion.

The materials are counted because the work belongs to the LORD.

This teaches that spiritual work still needs faithful stewardship.

The tabernacle is beautiful, but it is also accountable.

The gifts do not disappear into vagueness.

They become sockets, hooks, altar tools, courtyard pieces, and holy service.

## Exodus 39:1-31

**Scripture**

And of the blue, and purple, and scarlet, they made cloths of service, to do service in the holy place.

And they made the ephod of gold, blue, and purple, and scarlet.

And they set onyx stones enclosed in ouches of gold, graven, as signets are graven, with the names of the children of Israel.

And they made the plate of the holy crown of pure gold, and wrote upon it a writing, like to the engravings of a signet, HOLINESS TO THE LORD.

**Narrator**

Exodus 39 turns to the priestly garments.

This is not just clothing.

It is holy representation.

The priest stands before God on behalf of the people.

The names of Israel are carried on the shoulders.

And the names are carried over the heart.

That is a powerful picture.

The priest does not enter holy service as a private individual.

He carries the people before God.

Strength and affection are both pictured.

Shoulders.

Heart.

The robe, bells, pomegranates, tunics, turban, and holy crown all teach that priestly ministry is serious.

And the gold plate says:

Holiness to the LORD.

The priesthood is not personal status.

It belongs to God.

## Exodus 39:32-43

**Scripture**

Thus was all the work of the tabernacle of the tent of the congregation finished.

And the children of Israel did according to all that the LORD commanded Moses, so did they.

And Moses did look upon all the work, and, behold, they had done it as the LORD had commanded.

And Moses blessed them.

**Narrator**

This is one of the most beautiful turns in Exodus.

The work is finished.

The people bring everything to Moses.

And Moses inspects it.

He looks upon all the work.

And the verdict is clear:

They did it as the LORD commanded.

After the golden calf, that sentence is full of mercy.

These are the same people who failed badly.

They are not suddenly perfect.

But by God's mercy, failure is not the final word.

The people who once made false worship now complete commanded worship.

And Moses blesses them.

Their willing gifts and skilled labor have become obedience.

Their obedience has become worship.

## Exodus 40:1-16

**Scripture**

And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying, On the first day of the first month shalt thou set up the tabernacle.

And thou shalt put therein the ark of the testimony.

And thou shalt bring in the table, and set in order the things that are to be set in order upon it.

And thou shalt bring in the candlestick, and light the lamps thereof.

And thou shalt anoint the tabernacle, and all that is therein, and shalt hallow it.

And thou shalt bring Aaron and his sons unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, and wash them with water.

Thus did Moses: according to all that the LORD commanded him, so did he.

**Narrator**

Exodus 40 begins with setup.

The timing matters.

The first day of the first month.

It feels like a new beginning.

The pieces are not left in a pile.

The ark is placed.

The table is arranged.

The lampstand is lit.

The incense altar is set.

The bronze altar is placed.

The laver is filled.

The courtyard is raised.

The tabernacle and its contents are anointed.

Aaron and his sons are washed, clothed, and consecrated.

Everything that was designed, made, and inspected is now being put into place.

The tabernacle is not merely a completed project.

It is a dwelling prepared for the presence of God.

## Exodus 40:17-33

**Scripture**

And it came to pass in the first month in the second year, on the first day of the month, that the tabernacle was reared up.

And Moses reared up the tabernacle, and fastened his sockets, and set up the boards thereof.

And he took and put the testimony into the ark, and set the mercy seat above upon the ark.

And he put the table in the tent of the congregation, and set the bread in order upon it before the LORD.

And he lighted the lamps before the LORD.

And he burnt sweet incense thereon.

And he set the laver between the tent of the congregation and the altar, and put water there, to wash withal.

So Moses finished the work.

**Narrator**

Now Moses actually raises the tabernacle.

The instructions become visible.

The dwelling stands in the camp.

Again and again the text repeats the same idea:

As the LORD commanded Moses.

That repetition is not boring.

It is the point.

After the chaos of idolatry, Exodus slows down to show obedience in detail.

The ark goes behind the veil.

The testimony is inside.

The mercy seat is above.

The table holds bread.

The lampstand gives light.

Incense rises.

The altar is ready.

The laver holds water for washing.

The courtyard surrounds the dwelling.

Then comes the sentence:

So Moses finished the work.

Everything is ready.

The question now is whether God will fill it.

## Exodus 40:34-38

**Scripture**

Then a cloud covered the tent of the congregation, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle.

And Moses was not able to enter into the tent of the congregation, because the cloud abode thereon, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle.

And when the cloud was taken up from over the tabernacle, the children of Israel went onward in all their journeys.

But if the cloud were not taken up, then they journeyed not till the day that it was taken up.

For the cloud of the LORD was upon the tabernacle by day, and fire was on it by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel, throughout all their journeys.

**Narrator**

This is the climax of Exodus.

The cloud covers the tent.

The glory of the LORD fills the tabernacle.

God comes to dwell among the people He rescued.

The book began with Pharaoh filling Israel's life with burdens.

It ends with the LORD filling the tabernacle with glory.

Pharaoh said, Work for me.

God says, I will dwell among you.

Pharaoh tried to control Israel's bodies, sons, labor, and future.

God now places His presence in the middle of the camp.

But the ending is not casual.

Moses cannot enter because the glory is overwhelming.

God's nearness remains weighty.

The final verses also show that the journey is not over.

The cloud lifts when Israel should move.

The cloud stays when Israel should stay.

Fire is there by night.

So Exodus ends with presence and guidance.

The people are still in the wilderness.

They are not home yet.

But they are not alone.

The cloud is there.

The fire is there.

The glory is there.

And that changes everything.

## Closing Reflection

Day 31 shows the goal of Exodus.

God did not merely rescue Israel from Egypt so they could escape pain.

He rescued them so He could dwell among them.

That is bigger than freedom from Pharaoh.

It is life with God.

Exodus teaches us that worship should be shaped by God's word, not by human fear or imagination.

It teaches us that mercy belongs at the center.

It teaches us that sacrifice, cleansing, priesthood, obedience, generosity, and stewardship all matter in the presence of a holy God.

And it teaches us that failure does not have to be the final word when God gives mercy and restores obedience.

The book ends in the wilderness.

But it ends with God in the middle of His people.

That is the comfort.

The road continues.

But the LORD goes with them.
