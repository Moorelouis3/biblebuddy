# Bible in One Year - Day 46 Narrator Script

**Reading:** Numbers 30-33

## Opening

Welcome to Day 46 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Numbers chapters 30 through 33.

The theme is vows, victory, and the journey reviewed.

Israel is close to the land now.

These chapters teach that words matter, compromise has consequences, shared responsibility matters, and memory matters before moving forward.

## Numbers 30 verses 1 through 16

**Scripture**

Moses speaks to the heads of the tribes about vows.

If a man vows a vow unto the LORD, he must not break his word.

The chapter also gives instructions about vows made by daughters, wives, widows, and divorced women within household authority.

**Narrator**

A vow is a serious promise made before God.

Numbers 30 teaches that words are not disposable.

To vow something to the LORD and then treat it lightly is dangerous.

This chapter also speaks into household structures in ancient Israel.

Some details require careful reading, but the main point is clear.

🗣️ Words matter.

🤝 Promises matter.

🙏 Vows before God matter.

Covenant people must be truthful people.

## Numbers 31 verses 1 through 54

**Scripture**

The LORD commands Moses to avenge Israel of the Midianites.

Israel fights Midian.

Balaam is killed.

The spoil is purified and divided.

The officers bring an offering to the LORD because no men of war are missing.

**Narrator**

This chapter connects back to Baalpeor.

Midian helped draw Israel into idolatry and immorality.

So this battle is judgment tied to covenant corruption.

Balaam's death matters too.

He could not curse Israel with his mouth, but his influence is connected to Israel's compromise.

The purification of spoil reminds us that even victory must be handled before God.

Israel does not get to treat judgment, war, wealth, or survival casually.

## Numbers 32 verses 1 through 42

**Scripture**

The children of Reuben and Gad have much cattle.

They see that the land of Jazer and Gilead is good for livestock.

They ask to receive land east of Jordan.

Moses warns them not to discourage the rest of Israel.

They promise to go armed before their brethren until the land is possessed.

**Narrator**

This request could have become another moment like the spies.

Moses hears danger in it.

If these tribes settle early and stop fighting, the rest of Israel could lose courage.

So Reuben, Gad, and half Manasseh must promise to help their brothers.

Their families and livestock may stay east of Jordan, but the fighting men must cross over.

The lesson is clear.

Personal blessing must not cancel shared responsibility.

## Numbers 33 verses 1 through 49

**Scripture**

Moses records the journeys of the children of Israel.

The list begins when Israel went out of Egypt after Passover.

It names many stages from Rameses to the plains of Moab by Jordan near Jericho.

**Narrator**

This journey list can feel like a map full of unfamiliar names.

But it is more than geography.

It is memory.

Every stop is part of the story God carried them through.

Some places held water.

Some held testing.

Some held judgment.

Some held mercy.

The list says, in its own way, the LORD brought us this far.

Before entering the land, Israel must remember the road.

## Numbers 33 verses 50 through 56

**Scripture**

The LORD commands Israel to drive out the inhabitants of the land.

They must destroy pictures, molten images, and high places.

If they do not drive out the inhabitants, those who remain will be pricks in their eyes and thorns in their sides.

**Narrator**

The warning is about compromise.

Israel must not enter the land and keep the idolatry of Canaan as decoration.

False worship is not harmless.

If Israel leaves it in place, it will become a snare.

👁️ Pricks in the eyes

🌵 Thorns in the sides

⚠️ Trouble in the land

God is not being vague.

Partial obedience will bring painful consequences.

## Closing

That is Day 46.

Numbers 30 through 33 teaches that vows, victory, settlement, memory, and separation all matter before God.

The people are close to the land, but they still need faithful hearts.
