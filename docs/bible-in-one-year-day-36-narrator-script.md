# Bible In One Year - Day 36 Narrator Script

## Day 36 - Holy Living Before a Holy God

**Reading:** Leviticus 17 through 20
**Bible text:** King James Version, Leviticus 17-20
**Audio style:** One narrator, cinematic Bible teaching with Scripture reading

## Opening

Welcome to Day 36 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Leviticus 17 through 20.

Yesterday, Leviticus brought us to the Day of Atonement.

Blood was brought into the Most Holy Place.

One goat died.

One goat carried sin away.

Now the question becomes:

What does life look like for a people who live near a holy God?

Leviticus 17 through 20 answers that question.

Blood must be treated with reverence.

Worship must not drift into idolatry.

Desire must come under God's holiness.

Family boundaries must be protected.

Justice, speech, wages, harvest, strangers, elders, and business all matter to the LORD.

These chapters can feel intense, but the main idea is clear:

God is forming a people who belong to Him.

They are not to live like Egypt behind them.

They are not to live like Canaan ahead of them.

They are to be holy because the LORD their God is holy.

## Leviticus 17:1-7

Leviticus 17 verses 1 through 7.

**Scripture**

And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,

Speak unto Aaron, and unto his sons, and unto all the children of Israel, and say unto them; This is the thing which the LORD hath commanded, saying,

What man soever there be of the house of Israel, that killeth an ox, or lamb, or goat, in the camp, or that killeth it out of the camp,

And bringeth it not unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, to offer an offering unto the LORD before the tabernacle of the LORD; blood shall be imputed unto that man.

To the end that the children of Israel may bring their sacrifices, which they offer in the open field, even that they may bring them unto the LORD, unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, unto the priest.

And the priest shall sprinkle the blood upon the altar of the LORD at the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, and burn the fat for a sweet savour unto the LORD.

And they shall no more offer their sacrifices unto devils, after whom they have gone a whoring.

**Narrator**

Leviticus 17 begins by pulling sacrifice toward the tabernacle.

That may sound like a small detail, but it is not small.

Israel has a holy place where God has chosen to dwell.

So sacrifice cannot become private religion in the open field.

The animal must be brought to the door of the tabernacle.

The priest must handle the blood.

The fat is burned to the LORD.

Verse 7 shows why this matters.

Unauthorized sacrifice can drift into false worship.

Israel must not offer sacrifices to devils.

God is teaching them that worship belongs to Him.

It must come His way.

## Leviticus 17:8-16

Leviticus 17 verses 8 through 16.

**Scripture**

Whatsoever man there be of the house of Israel, or of the strangers which sojourn among you, that offereth a burnt offering or sacrifice,

And bringeth it not unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, to offer it unto the LORD; even that man shall be cut off from among his people.

And whatsoever man there be of the house of Israel, or of the strangers that sojourn among you, that eateth any manner of blood; I will even set my face against that soul that eateth blood.

For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls.

Therefore I said unto the children of Israel, No soul of you shall eat blood.

And whatsoever man there be which hunteth and catcheth any beast or fowl that may be eaten; he shall even pour out the blood thereof, and cover it with dust.

For it is the life of all flesh; the blood of it is for the life thereof.

**Narrator**

This is one of the most important blood passages in Leviticus.

The life of the flesh is in the blood.

That sentence explains why blood is treated so carefully.

Blood represents life.

Life belongs to God.

So blood must not be treated like common food.

God also says He has given blood upon the altar to make atonement for the soul.

That word given matters.

Atonement is not something Israel invents.

God provides it.

Even hunting is brought under this truth.

When an animal is hunted for food, the blood is poured out and covered with dust.

Ordinary meals still happen under God's ownership of life.

## Leviticus 18:1-5

Leviticus 18 verses 1 through 5.

**Scripture**

And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,

Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, I am the LORD your God.

After the doings of the land of Egypt, wherein ye dwelt, shall ye not do.

And after the doings of the land of Canaan, whither I bring you, shall ye not do.

Neither shall ye walk in their ordinances.

Ye shall do my judgments, and keep mine ordinances, to walk therein: I am the LORD your God.

Ye shall therefore keep my statutes, and my judgments: which if a man do, he shall live in them: I am the LORD.

**Narrator**

Leviticus 18 begins with identity.

I am the LORD your God.

That means the commands that follow are not random moral opinions.

They come from the God who rescued Israel and now dwells among them.

Israel must not copy Egypt.

That is where they came from.

Israel must not copy Canaan.

That is where they are going.

God's people can live in a place without becoming like that place.

Their moral compass must be God's word, not the culture around them.

## Leviticus 18:6-23

Leviticus 18 verses 6 through 23.

**Scripture**

None of you shall approach to any that is near of kin to him, to uncover their nakedness: I am the LORD.

The nakedness of thy father, or the nakedness of thy mother, shalt thou not uncover.

The nakedness of thy sister, the daughter of thy father, or daughter of thy mother, even their nakedness thou shalt not uncover.

Thou shalt not uncover the nakedness of thy daughter in law: she is thy son's wife.

Thou shalt not uncover the nakedness of thy brother's wife: it is thy brother's nakedness.

Moreover thou shalt not lie carnally with thy neighbour's wife, to defile thyself with her.

And thou shalt not let any of thy seed pass through the fire to Molech, neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God: I am the LORD.

Thou shalt not lie with mankind, as with womankind: it is abomination.

Neither shalt thou lie with any beast to defile thyself therewith: it is confusion.

**Narrator**

This section is serious because it deals with desire, family, marriage, children, and the body.

The repeated phrase is uncover nakedness.

That means a forbidden sexual relationship.

God names close family relationships because closeness can be abused.

Family nearness must be protected.

It must not become permission for violation.

The chapter also names adultery, Molech worship, same-sex relations, and bestiality.

Molech worship involved children and false worship.

It is placed here because idolatry and moral corruption often grow together.

The main lesson is not that bodies are bad.

The lesson is that bodies belong under God's holiness.

## Leviticus 18:24-30

Leviticus 18 verses 24 through 30.

**Scripture**

Defile not ye yourselves in any of these things.

For in all these the nations are defiled which I cast out before you.

And the land is defiled: therefore I do visit the iniquity thereof upon it, and the land itself vomiteth out her inhabitants.

Ye shall therefore keep my statutes and my judgments, and shall not commit any of these abominations.

That the land spue not you out also, when ye defile it, as it spued out the nations that were before you.

Therefore shall ye keep mine ordinance, that ye commit not any one of these abominable customs, which were committed before you, and that ye defile not yourselves therein: I am the LORD your God.

**Narrator**

Leviticus 18 ends with a warning about defilement.

Sin does not stay invisible.

The nations were defiled by these practices.

The land itself is described as defiled.

Then Leviticus uses a strong picture.

The land vomits out its inhabitants.

That language is meant to make us feel the seriousness of corruption.

Israel must not assume they are safe if they copy the sins God judged.

The promised land is a gift.

But it must not be filled with the same defilement.

## Leviticus 19:1-10

Leviticus 19 verses 1 through 10.

**Scripture**

And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,

Speak unto all the congregation of the children of Israel, and say unto them, Ye shall be holy: for I the LORD your God am holy.

Ye shall fear every man his mother, and his father, and keep my sabbaths: I am the LORD your God.

Turn ye not unto idols, nor make to yourselves molten gods: I am the LORD your God.

And if ye offer a sacrifice of peace offerings unto the LORD, ye shall offer it at your own will.

And when ye reap the harvest of your land, thou shalt not wholly reap the corners of thy field, neither shalt thou gather the gleanings of thy harvest.

And thou shalt not glean thy vineyard, neither shalt thou gather every grape of thy vineyard; thou shalt leave them for the poor and stranger: I am the LORD your God.

**Narrator**

Leviticus 19 is one of the clearest chapters in the Bible for practical holiness.

Ye shall be holy.

Why?

Because the LORD your God is holy.

God's character becomes the pattern for His people.

Then the chapter immediately moves into normal life.

Honor mother and father.

Keep Sabbath.

Reject idols.

Offer peace offerings rightly.

Leave part of the harvest for the poor and the stranger.

That last command is beautiful.

God builds mercy into the field.

The edges of the harvest are not squeezed for maximum profit.

They become provision for the vulnerable.

## Leviticus 19:11-18

Leviticus 19 verses 11 through 18.

**Scripture**

Ye shall not steal, neither deal falsely, neither lie one to another.

And ye shall not swear by my name falsely, neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God: I am the LORD.

Thou shalt not defraud thy neighbour, neither rob him.

The wages of him that is hired shall not abide with thee all night until the morning.

Thou shalt not curse the deaf, nor put a stumblingblock before the blind, but shalt fear thy God: I am the LORD.

Ye shall do no unrighteousness in judgment.

Thou shalt not respect the person of the poor, nor honour the person of the mighty: but in righteousness shalt thou judge thy neighbour.

Thou shalt not go up and down as a talebearer among thy people.

Thou shalt not hate thy brother in thine heart.

Thou shalt not avenge, nor bear any grudge against the children of thy people, but thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself: I am the LORD.

**Narrator**

This section shows how practical holiness becomes.

Do not steal.

Do not lie.

Do not use God's name to support a false oath.

Do not hold back a worker's wages overnight.

Do not curse the deaf.

Do not place a stumblingblock before the blind.

God sees the vulnerable person even when other people do not.

Justice must not favor the poor just because they are poor.

Justice must not favor the mighty because they are powerful.

Speech must not become slander.

Hidden hatred must not grow in the heart.

Revenge and grudges must not rule the community.

Then comes the famous command:

Love thy neighbour as thyself.

Love is not separate from holiness.

Love is part of holiness.

## Leviticus 19:19-37

Leviticus 19 verses 19 through 37.

**Scripture**

Ye shall keep my statutes.

Thou shalt not sow thy field with mingled seed: neither shall a garment mingled of linen and woollen come upon thee.

And he shall bring his trespass offering unto the LORD, unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, even a ram for a trespass offering.

And the priest shall make an atonement for him with the ram of the trespass offering before the LORD.

And when ye shall come into the land, and shall have planted all manner of trees for food, then ye shall count the fruit thereof as uncircumcised: three years it shall not be eaten of.

But in the fourth year all the fruit thereof shall be holy to praise the LORD withal.

Ye shall not eat any thing with the blood: neither shall ye use enchantment, nor observe times.

Ye shall not make any cuttings in your flesh for the dead, nor print any marks upon you: I am the LORD.

Ye shall keep my sabbaths, and reverence my sanctuary: I am the LORD.

Regard not them that have familiar spirits, neither seek after wizards, to be defiled by them.

Thou shalt rise up before the hoary head, and honour the face of the old man, and fear thy God: I am the LORD.

And if a stranger sojourn with thee in your land, ye shall not vex him.

But the stranger that dwelleth with you shall be unto you as one born among you, and thou shalt love him as thyself; for ye were strangers in the land of Egypt: I am the LORD your God.

Just balances, just weights, a just ephah, and a just hin, shall ye have.

Therefore shall ye observe all my statutes, and all my judgments, and do them: I am the LORD.

**Narrator**

This part of Leviticus 19 may feel like many commands placed together.

But the thread is clear.

Holiness reaches ordinary life.

It reaches fields.

It reaches clothing.

It reaches difficult moral cases.

It reaches fruit trees and patience in the land.

It reaches blood, pagan practices, mourning, bodies, Sabbath, and sanctuary.

It reaches older people.

It reaches strangers.

It reaches business weights and measures.

Israel must love the stranger because they were strangers in Egypt.

That is powerful.

People rescued by God must not forget what it felt like to be vulnerable.

## Leviticus 20:1-8

Leviticus 20 verses 1 through 8.

**Scripture**

Again, thou shalt say to the children of Israel, Whosoever he be of the children of Israel, or of the strangers that sojourn in Israel, that giveth any of his seed unto Molech; he shall surely be put to death.

And I will set my face against that man, and will cut him off from among his people.

Because he hath given of his seed unto Molech, to defile my sanctuary, and to profane my holy name.

And if the people of the land do any ways hide their eyes from the man, when he giveth of his seed unto Molech,

Then I will set my face against that man, and against his family.

And the soul that turneth after such as have familiar spirits, and after wizards, to go a whoring after them, I will even set my face against that soul.

Sanctify yourselves therefore, and be ye holy: for I am the LORD your God.

And ye shall keep my statutes, and do them: I am the LORD which sanctify you.

**Narrator**

Leviticus 20 is heavy because it focuses on consequences.

The chapter begins with Molech worship.

This was a horrifying false worship practice involving children.

God says it defiles His sanctuary and profanes His holy name.

The community must not hide its eyes from this evil.

That phrase matters.

Ignoring evil is not the same as being peaceful.

It allows defilement to continue.

God also forbids familiar spirits and wizards.

Israel must not chase forbidden spiritual power.

They are to sanctify themselves and be holy because the LORD sanctifies them.

## Leviticus 20:9-21

Leviticus 20 verses 9 through 21.

**Scripture**

For every one that curseth his father or his mother shall be surely put to death.

And the man that committeth adultery with another man's wife, even he that committeth adultery with his neighbour's wife, the adulterer and the adulteress shall surely be put to death.

And the man that lieth with his father's wife hath uncovered his father's nakedness.

And if a man lie with his daughter in law, both of them shall surely be put to death: they have wrought confusion.

If a man also lie with mankind, as he lieth with a woman, both of them have committed an abomination.

And if a man take a wife and her mother, it is wickedness.

And if a man shall take his sister, it is a wicked thing.

And if a man shall lie with a woman having her sickness, they shall be cut off from among their people.

And thou shalt not uncover the nakedness of thy mother's sister, nor of thy father's sister: for he uncovereth his near kin.

They shall bear their iniquity.

**Narrator**

This section repeats many boundaries from Leviticus 18.

But now the focus is consequence.

The language is severe because the sins are severe.

Family dishonor matters.

Marriage matters.

Family boundaries matter.

The body matters.

Blood and uncleanness matter.

God is teaching Israel that sin is not weightless.

Some sins damage the whole covenant community.

Modern readers may feel the heaviness of this chapter, and we should.

Leviticus is not asking us to treat holiness like decoration.

It is showing that life with the holy God is serious.

## Leviticus 20:22-27

Leviticus 20 verses 22 through 27.

**Scripture**

Ye shall therefore keep all my statutes, and all my judgments, and do them: that the land, whither I bring you to dwell therein, spue you not out.

And ye shall not walk in the manners of the nation, which I cast out before you.

But I have said unto you, Ye shall inherit their land, and I will give it unto you to possess it, a land that floweth with milk and honey.

I am the LORD your God, which have separated you from other people.

Ye shall therefore put difference between clean beasts and unclean, and between unclean fowls and clean.

And ye shall be holy unto me: for I the LORD am holy, and have severed you from other people, that ye should be mine.

A man also or woman that hath a familiar spirit, or that is a wizard, shall surely be put to death.

**Narrator**

The final section gives the heart of the reading.

Israel must keep God's statutes so the land does not spue them out.

They must not walk in the manners of the nations.

God is giving them a land flowing with milk and honey.

That is gift language.

The land is good.

But they must live in it as God's holy people.

Then verse 26 gives the purpose:

that ye should be mine.

Do not miss that.

Holiness is not only about what Israel avoids.

Holiness is about whom Israel belongs to.

God separates His people so they can be His.

## Closing

Leviticus 17 through 20 teaches that holiness is the whole shape of life before God.

Blood belongs to God.

Worship belongs to God.

The body belongs to God.

Family belongs to God.

Justice belongs to God.

Speech belongs to God.

Business belongs to God.

The stranger belongs under God's care.

The land belongs under God's holiness.

And God's people belong to Him.

That is the heart of Day 36.

Be holy because the LORD is holy.

Live differently because you belong to Him.
