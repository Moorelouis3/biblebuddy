# Bible in One Year - Day 48 Narrator Script

**Reading:** Deuteronomy 2-5

## Opening

Welcome to Day 48 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Deuteronomy chapters 2 through 5.

The theme is remembering the journey and the covenant.

Moses is speaking to the new generation.

He wants them to remember where God led them, what God gave them, and how God spoke to them.

## Deuteronomy 2 verses 1 through 37

**Scripture**

Moses remembers Israel turning into the wilderness by the way of the Red Sea.

God tells them not to meddle with Edom, Moab, or Ammon, because He gave those lands to others.

Then Sihon king of Heshbon refuses passage and fights Israel.

The LORD gives Sihon into Israel's hand.

**Narrator**

This chapter teaches restraint and victory.

Israel cannot take whatever land they want.

God gave some lands to other peoples.

That means obedience sometimes means not taking.

But when Sihon resists, the LORD gives victory.

🚫 Lands Israel must not seize

⚔️ Battle God gives

🧭 Journey God directs

The LORD is not only powerful in battle.

He is also Lord over boundaries.

## Deuteronomy 3 verses 1 through 29

**Scripture**

Moses remembers victory over Og king of Bashan.

The land east of Jordan is given to Reuben, Gad, and half Manasseh.

Moses commands Joshua to remember what the LORD has done and not fear.

Moses pleads to enter the land, but the LORD tells him to go up Pisgah and see it with his eyes.

**Narrator**

Og was a terrifying enemy.

But the LORD gave victory.

Moses uses that memory to strengthen Joshua.

What God has done becomes courage for what God will do.

Moses also tells his own painful story.

He wants to enter the land, but the LORD says no.

He may see it, but Joshua will lead the people in.

The mission continues, even when Moses' role changes.

## Deuteronomy 4 verses 1 through 24

**Scripture**

Moses calls Israel to hearken to the statutes and judgments of the LORD.

They must not add to or take away from God's word.

They must remember Horeb, where they heard God's voice but saw no form.

They must not make graven images.

The LORD is a consuming fire, even a jealous God.

**Narrator**

Moses connects memory to obedience.

Israel heard God's voice.

They did not see a shape.

That matters because they must not make an image and pretend it represents God.

God is not like the idols of the nations.

He cannot be reduced to metal, wood, stone, sun, moon, or stars.

The phrase consuming fire teaches holiness and seriousness.

God's covenant love is not casual.

## Deuteronomy 4 verses 25 through 49

**Scripture**

Moses warns that if Israel corrupts itself with idols, they will be scattered among the nations.

But if they seek the LORD with all their heart and soul, they will find Him.

Moses says the LORD is God in heaven above and on earth beneath; there is none else.

Then cities of refuge east of Jordan are named.

**Narrator**

This warning reaches far into Israel's future.

Moses speaks of idolatry, exile, and mercy.

If Israel turns away, scattering will come.

But exile will not make God impossible to seek.

If they seek Him with all the heart and soul, He will be found.

This shows both warning and mercy.

God is holy enough to judge idolatry and merciful enough to receive the repentant.

## Deuteronomy 5 verses 1 through 33

**Scripture**

Moses calls all Israel to hear the statutes and judgments.

He repeats the Ten Commandments.

He reminds Israel how they heard the voice of the LORD from the fire.

The people feared and asked Moses to hear for them.

The LORD desired that they would have a heart to fear Him and keep His commandments always.

**Narrator**

Deuteronomy 5 gives the covenant words to the new generation.

They must not think Horeb only belonged to their parents.

These commandments are for them too.

The law begins with grace.

I am the LORD thy God, which brought thee out of the land of Egypt.

Obedience comes after rescue.

God wants more than rule-keeping.

He says, O that there were such an heart in them.

The covenant aims at the heart.

## Closing

That is Day 48.

Deuteronomy 2 through 5 teaches Israel to remember rightly.

God directed the road.

God gave victory.

God warned against idols.

God spoke His covenant words.

Memory is meant to become obedience.
