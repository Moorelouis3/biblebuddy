# Day 98 Narrator Script

## Opening

Welcome to Day 98 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading First Chronicles 17 through 20.

The title for today is David's Covenant and Victories.

This day begins with David wanting to build a house for the LORD.

But the LORD turns the promise around.

David will not build God's house.

The LORD will build David a house.

The covenant promise reaches beyond David's own lifetime and points toward a throne established forever.

After that, Chronicles records David's victories and the strength of the kingdom under God's help.

Day 98 teaches that God's promise is greater than David's plan.

## First Chronicles 17

First Chronicles 17 opens with David dwelling in his house while the ark remains under curtains.

David wants to build a house for God.

At first Nathan tells him to do all that is in his heart.

But that night the word of God comes to Nathan.

David will not build the house.

The LORD reminds David that He took him from the sheepcote, from following sheep, to be ruler over Israel.

God has been with him.

God has cut off his enemies.

God has made him a name.

Then the LORD promises to appoint a place for His people and subdue enemies.

Most importantly, He says He will build David a house.

David's seed will be established.

His throne will be established forever.

David responds with humility.

Who am I, O LORD God, and what is mine house?

He sits before the LORD and receives the promise in worship.

This chapter is central because it carries the Davidic covenant.

The hope of the kingdom rests on what God promises, not what David achieves.

## First Chronicles 18

First Chronicles 18 describes David's victories.

He defeats Philistines, Moabites, Zobah, Syrians, and Edomites.

The repeated line is that the LORD preserves David wherever he goes.

That line is the interpretation of the chapter.

David's success is not merely military skill.

The LORD is preserving him.

David also takes gold, brass, and tribute, and dedicates treasures to the LORD.

Victory becomes preparation for worship.

The chapter ends with David reigning over all Israel, executing judgment and justice among all his people.

That is the kingly ideal.

Power is meant to serve justice.

The kingdom is strong, but Chronicles keeps pointing back to the LORD's preserving hand.

## First Chronicles 19

First Chronicles 19 begins with David showing kindness to Hanun, son of Nahash, because Nahash had showed kindness to David.

But Hanun's princes suspect David's servants are spies.

They shame the servants by shaving them and cutting their garments.

The insult leads to war.

The Ammonites hire Syrian chariots and horsemen.

Joab sees the battle set against him before and behind.

He divides the forces and tells Abishai to be of good courage.

They must behave themselves valiantly for their people and the cities of God, and the LORD will do what is good in His sight.

That line matters.

Human courage and trust in God's will stand together.

The Syrians flee.

The Ammonites withdraw.

The enemies eventually make peace and will not help Ammon anymore.

## First Chronicles 20

First Chronicles 20 continues the account of battle.

Rabbah is besieged.

David takes the crown of the king from Rabbah, and it is placed on his head.

The chapter also records battles with Philistine giants.

The sons of the giant fall by the hand of David and his servants.

This reminds the reader of the earlier giant story with David and Goliath.

The kingdom continues to face enemies, but the LORD gives victory through David's house and servants.

Chronicles tells the story briefly, focusing on the stability and triumph of David's reign.

## Full Lesson Review

Day 98 is built around promise and preservation.

David wants to build for God, but God promises to build David's house.

David responds with humility and prayer.

The following battles show the LORD preserving David wherever he goes.

David dedicates spoil to the LORD and reigns with judgment and justice.

The lesson is that the kingdom rests on God's covenant promise and God's preserving power.

## Slow Walk

Listen closely to David's humility.

He does not treat God's refusal as rejection.

He receives God's better promise.

That is important for beginners.

Sometimes God says no to one plan because He is revealing something larger.

David wanted to build a temple.

God promised a dynasty.

David wanted to make a house for God.

God promised to make a house for David.

The story is bigger than David's intention because grace is bigger than David's plan.

## Deeper Teaching

First Chronicles 17 is one of the great promise chapters in the Old Testament.

David looks at his own house and then looks at the ark under curtains.

His desire to build sounds honorable.

Nathan even encourages him at first.

But God's word redirects the whole moment.

The LORD reminds David that He has been the builder of David's life.

God took David from following sheep.

God made him ruler.

God was with him.

God cut off his enemies.

God made him a name.

Before David can build anything for God, he must remember what God has already built for him.

Then the promise grows larger.

The LORD will appoint a place for His people.

The LORD will subdue enemies.

The LORD will build David a house.

The seed of David will sit on the throne.

The throne will be established forever.

This promise becomes one of the great roots of messianic hope.

For a beginner, the simple point is this: God's promise to David reaches beyond David.

It points forward to a kingdom God Himself will establish.

David's prayer is also important.

He sits before the LORD.

He asks, Who am I?

He receives grace with humility.

He does not boast that he earned the promise.

He worships because God has spoken.

First Chronicles 18 then shows the LORD preserving David.

The victories are not disconnected from the covenant.

The kingdom is being established.

Enemies are subdued.

Treasures are dedicated to the LORD.

David executes judgment and justice.

That is what a king should do.

First Chronicles 19 shows conflict beginning from suspicion and shame.

David sends kindness, but Hanun's princes interpret it as spying.

They humiliate David's servants.

Their fearful interpretation creates war.

Joab's words are the key: be of good courage, and let the LORD do that which is good in His sight.

That is not passive.

Joab fights.

But he fights under God's sovereignty.

First Chronicles 20 shows enemies still falling, including descendants of giants.

Chronicles is showing a kingdom secured by God's preserving hand.

David's covenant promise is followed by David's victories because God is making His word stand.

## Extended Narration

Day 98 stands near the center of the whole Old Testament story because First Chronicles 17 gives the promise to David.

David wants to build a house for the LORD.

On the surface, that sounds like a noble plan.

David is no longer hiding in caves.

He is no longer running from Saul.

He is living in his own house, and he looks at the ark under curtains.

He wants God's house to be honored.

But the LORD sends Nathan back with a different word.

David will not build God a house.

God will build David a house.

That reversal is one of the most important lessons in the passage.

God is not dependent on David's greatness.

David's greatness depends on God's grace.

The LORD reminds David that He took him from the sheepcote.

David did not climb into covenant significance by his own strength.

God took him.

God was with him.

God cut off his enemies.

God made him a name.

Before David can think about what he will do for God, he must hear what God has already done for him.

Then the promise reaches beyond David's own lifetime.

God says He will raise up David's seed.

He will establish his kingdom.

He will establish his throne for ever.

That promise points beyond Solomon, because Solomon's reign was real but not forever in the full sense.

The Bible keeps carrying this promise forward until it reaches Jesus Christ, the Son of David whose kingdom does not end.

Chronicles is not only interested in architecture.

It is interested in covenant hope.

The temple matters, but the promise behind the king matters even more.

David's response is worship.

He sits before the LORD and says, Who am I, O LORD God?

That is not false humility.

David understands that grace has lifted him.

He knows the promise is bigger than his own worth.

He receives God's word with wonder.

Then chapters 18 through 20 show victories around David.

At first, that can feel like a separate section about battles.

But Chronicles is placing the battles after the covenant promise for a reason.

God's word is not floating above real life.

God's promise works itself out in history.

Enemies are subdued.

Tribute comes in.

David reigns and executes judgment and justice.

The kingdom is being established because God said He would establish it.

The reader should also notice that victory does not erase dependence.

David still needs God's hand.

The people still need righteous order.

The kingdom still belongs to the LORD.

The promise is not permission for pride.

It is a call to trust the God who builds what human hands cannot secure.

For a beginner, Day 98 teaches that God's covenant promise is stronger than David's best idea.

David wants to honor God with a building.

God honors His own promise by building a royal line.

David offers service.

God gives grace.

David fights battles.

God establishes the kingdom.

And underneath the whole reading is the steady truth that the Bible is moving toward a King greater than David.

That King will not only win battles around Israel.

He will bring the kingdom of God.

He will reign forever.

He will fulfill the promise that David received with awe.

## Final Guided Recap

Day 98 is about promise before performance.

David wants to give God a house.

God gives David a promise.

David wants to build.

God says He will build.

Then the battles show that God's promise is not an empty speech.

The LORD preserves David wherever he goes.

The kingdom expands.

Spoil is dedicated.

Justice is executed.

Enemies are subdued.

The listener should remember that the center of the day is not David's greatness by itself.

The center is the LORD's covenant faithfulness.

David's humility teaches us how to receive grace.

The victories teach us how God upholds His word.

## Chapter Listening Guide

In chapter 17, listen for God reversing David's plan.

David wants to build a house for the LORD.

The LORD promises to build David a house.

That reversal teaches grace.

God is not dependent on David's gift.

David is dependent on God's promise.

In chapter 18, listen for preservation.

The repeated idea is that the LORD preserves David.

Victories, treasures, garrisons, tribute, and justice all sit under that explanation.

David's reign succeeds because the LORD is keeping His promise.

In chapter 19, listen for courage under pressure.

An act of intended kindness is misread as spying.

Shame leads to war.

Joab's charge teaches active trust: be courageous, fight faithfully, and leave the outcome in the LORD's hands.

In chapter 20, listen for the continuation of victory.

Enemies still rise.

Giants still appear.

But the kingdom is not abandoned.

The LORD continues to give victory through David and his servants.

The promise of chapter 17 echoes underneath the battles.

## Closing

As you finish Day 98, remember the promise: I will build thee an house.

David's hope rests not in what he can build for God, but in what God promises to build.
