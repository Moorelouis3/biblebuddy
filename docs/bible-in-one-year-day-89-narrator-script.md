# Day 89 Narrator Script

## Opening

Welcome to Day 89 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Second Kings 6 through 9.

The title for today is Rescue, Siege, and Jehu's Judgment.

This day moves through some of the most dramatic scenes in Kings.

An axe head floats.

Elisha's servant sees horses and chariots of fire.

Samaria suffers a terrible siege.

Four lepers discover that the LORD has scattered the Syrian army.

The Shunammite woman receives her land back.

Hazael rises in Syria.

Judah is entangled with Ahab's house.

Jehu is anointed to strike the house of Ahab.

The day is full of rescue and judgment side by side.

## Second Kings 6

Second Kings 6 begins with the sons of the prophets needing more room.

They go to Jordan to cut wood.

One man's borrowed axe head falls into the water.

He cries out because it was borrowed.

Elisha cuts down a stick, casts it into the water, and the iron swims.

This small miracle matters.

God's care is not only for national emergencies.

He sees ordinary distress too.

Then the king of Syria wars against Israel.

Elisha repeatedly warns the king of Israel where Syria is planning to camp.

The Syrian king thinks there is a traitor, but one of his servants says Elisha tells the king of Israel the words spoken in the bedchamber.

The Syrians surround Dothan to seize Elisha.

Elisha's servant is afraid.

Elisha prays, LORD, open his eyes.

The servant sees the mountain full of horses and chariots of fire round about Elisha.

They that be with us are more than they that be with them.

Elisha prays, and the Syrians are struck with blindness.

He leads them into Samaria.

Instead of killing them, the king feeds them and sends them away.

Mercy wins a surprising victory.

But later Ben-hadad besieges Samaria.

The famine becomes horrific.

The king hears a terrible story between two women and tears his clothes.

Then he blames Elisha and seeks his life.

The chapter ends with desperation pressing against the word of God.

## Second Kings 7

Second Kings 7 opens with Elisha announcing impossible mercy.

By tomorrow, food will be abundant in the gate of Samaria.

An officer doubts it.

Even if the LORD made windows in heaven, could this thing be?

Elisha says he will see it with his eyes but not eat of it.

Four leprous men sit at the entering of the gate.

They reason that staying means death, going into the city means death, and going to the Syrian camp might mean life.

When they arrive, the camp is empty.

The LORD had made the Syrians hear the noise of chariots, horses, and a great host.

They fled in fear and left tents, food, silver, gold, and raiment.

The lepers eat and hide treasure, but then they say, We do not well.

This is a day of good tidings.

They go and tell the city.

The king suspects a trap, but servants investigate and find the road full of garments and vessels the Syrians threw away.

The people go out and spoil the tents.

Food becomes abundant, just as Elisha said.

The doubting officer is trampled in the gate and dies, seeing the mercy but not eating it.

The chapter teaches that the LORD can turn famine into abundance overnight.

His word is not limited by what looks possible.

## Second Kings 8

Second Kings 8 begins with the Shunammite woman.

Elisha had warned her of a seven-year famine.

She went away, and when she returns, she appeals to the king for her house and land.

At that exact moment, Gehazi is telling the king about Elisha raising her son.

The king restores all that was hers.

The timing shows God's care.

Then Elisha comes to Damascus.

Ben-hadad is sick and sends Hazael to ask whether he will recover.

Elisha says Ben-hadad may recover, but the LORD has shown that he will surely die.

Elisha weeps because he sees the evil Hazael will do to Israel.

Hazael asks, Is thy servant a dog, that he should do this great thing?

But he returns, smothers Ben-hadad, and becomes king.

The chapter then turns to Judah.

Jehoram of Judah walks in the way of the kings of Israel because he is connected by marriage to Ahab's house.

Yet the LORD will not destroy Judah for David's sake.

That covenant mercy matters.

Ahaziah also walks in the way of Ahab's house.

Judah's royal line is now dangerously entangled with the northern kingdom's wickedness.

## Second Kings 9

Second Kings 9 begins with Elisha sending one of the sons of the prophets to anoint Jehu.

Jehu is told to strike the house of Ahab, so the blood of the prophets and servants of the LORD may be avenged at the hand of Jezebel.

The word against Ahab's house is now moving into judgment.

Jehu's companions spread garments under him and blow the trumpet, saying, Jehu is king.

Jehu drives furiously toward Jezreel.

Joram of Israel and Ahaziah of Judah come out to meet him.

Joram asks, Is it peace?

Jehu answers, What peace, so long as the whoredoms of thy mother Jezebel and her witchcrafts are so many?

Joram turns to flee, but Jehu shoots him.

Joram's body is cast in Naboth's field, according to the word of the LORD.

Ahaziah is also struck.

Then Jehu comes to Jezreel.

Jezebel paints her face, tires her head, and looks out at a window.

Jehu commands that she be thrown down.

Her blood is sprinkled on the wall and horses, and he treads her under foot.

When they go to bury her, they find only the skull, feet, and palms of her hands.

The word of the LORD spoken by Elijah is fulfilled.

## Full Lesson Review

Day 89 shows that God's word sees both hidden danger and future judgment.

The servant's eyes are opened to see heavenly protection.

Samaria's famine is answered by the LORD scattering an army.

The Shunammite receives restoration.

Hazael's violence is foreseen.

Judah is preserved for David's sake, even while its kings drift toward Ahab's house.

Jehu brings judgment on Ahab's line.

This day is not gentle, but it is clear.

The LORD rescues, warns, restores, and judges.

## Slow Walk Through the Day

Day 89 holds together ordinary need and national crisis.

The borrowed axe head is a small problem compared with siege and royal judgment, but the Bible still includes it.

That matters.

The LORD's care is not only for events that look historically massive.

He sees a servant distressed over something borrowed.

The floating iron reminds the listener that God can care for the small losses of ordinary people.

Then the scene widens.

The Syrian army surrounds Dothan, and Elisha's servant sees only danger.

Elisha prays for opened eyes.

The horses and chariots of fire were already there before the servant saw them.

The prayer does not create God's protection.

It reveals what fear could not see.

That is one of the great lessons of the chapter.

Sight is not the same as reality.

Faith learns that the LORD's invisible help is greater than visible threat.

The siege of Samaria then shows the horror of life under judgment and enemy pressure.

The famine is not described politely.

The story becomes almost unbearable.

This is what broken leadership, sin, and war do to a people.

Second Kings 7 answers that darkness with unexpected mercy.

The good news comes first through lepers outside the city.

They are not important officials.

They are desperate men who discover abundance because the LORD has already acted.

Their sentence, We do not well, is important.

Good news is not meant to be hidden.

They must tell the city.

Second Kings 8 restores the Shunammite woman and then shows Hazael rising.

The same chapter can contain personal restoration and national sorrow.

Elisha weeps because he sees what Hazael will do.

The prophet is not cold toward judgment.

He feels the pain of what is coming.

Second Kings 9 brings Jehu's anointing and Ahab's house into judgment.

Joram falls in Naboth's field.

Jezebel dies according to Elijah's word.

The location and details matter because they show that God's word has remembered injustice.

Naboth's blood was not forgotten.

The prophets killed under Jezebel were not forgotten.

Judgment may seem delayed, but it is not lost.

## Prayerful Landing

Day 89 asks the listener to trust the LORD's sight.

The servant needed his eyes opened.

The king of Israel needed to see that Elisha was not the enemy.

The starving city needed to learn that the LORD could provide when no human plan remained.

Hazael needed to see the evil that was in his future, though he denied it at first.

Jehu needed to see his calling under the word of the LORD.

And the reader needs to see that God keeps account.

He sees borrowed tools.

He sees surrounded servants.

He sees starving cities.

He sees grieving women.

He sees hidden violence.

He sees Naboth's field.

The day is heavy, but not hopeless.

The LORD who judges also rescues.

The LORD who exposes evil also restores what was lost.

The LORD who opens eyes can teach His people to stop measuring reality by fear alone.

## Final Guided Recap

Before the closing, gather the movements of this day.

Second Kings 6 begins small and becomes enormous.

The floating axe head shows care for ordinary loss.

The horses and chariots of fire show protection beyond human sight.

The blinded Syrians show that the LORD can defeat enemies without ordinary battle.

The siege of Samaria shows how terrible judgment and war become when a city is trapped.

Second Kings 7 answers famine with impossible abundance.

Elisha's word sounds unbelievable to the officer, but the LORD is not limited by human imagination.

Four lepers discover what the city does not yet know: God has already scattered the enemy.

The good news must be shared.

Second Kings 8 restores the Shunammite and then turns toward Hazael's rise.

Personal mercy and national sorrow stand beside each other.

God can restore one woman's land while also revealing hard judgment coming through Syria.

Second Kings 9 brings Jehu's anointing and Ahab's house into judgment.

Joram falls in Naboth's field.

Jezebel's end fulfills the word spoken through Elijah.

The chapter is severe because the injustice was severe.

The LORD did not forget Naboth.

He did not forget the prophets.

He did not forget His word.

Day 89 calls the listener to trust what God sees, even when the visible scene looks confusing.

Fear sees only the army around the city.

Faith learns to ask the LORD to open the eyes.

## Closing

As you finish Day 89, remember the mountain full of horses and chariots of fire.

God's people may feel surrounded, but the LORD sees more than fear can see.

They that be with us are more than they that be with them.
