# Bible In One Year - Day 17 Narrator Script

## Day 17 - Joseph Rises And Remembers

**Reading:** Genesis 41 and 42  
**Bible text:** King James Version, Genesis 41-42  
**Audio style:** Narrator teaching with Scripture reading  

## Opening

Welcome to Day 17 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Genesis 41 and 42.

Day 16 ended with Joseph still in prison.

He had interpreted the cupbearer's dream, asked to be remembered, and then the cupbearer forgot him.

Genesis 41 begins with two full years passing.

That is important.

Joseph is gifted, but still waiting.

Joseph is faithful, but still unseen.

Joseph helped someone else, but still has not been helped.

Then Pharaoh dreams.

And in one day, Joseph is brought out of the dungeon, stands before Pharaoh, gives God the credit, interprets the dream, and is raised over Egypt.

But Genesis 42 shows that Joseph's rise is not only about Joseph.

Famine reaches Canaan, and Joseph's brothers come to Egypt for bread.

They bow before the brother they sold.

They do not recognize him.

But Joseph recognizes them.

And suddenly the old dreams, the old guilt, and the old family wound all come back into the room.

## Genesis 41:1-8

**Scripture**

1. And it came to pass at the end of two full years, that Pharaoh dreamed: and, behold, he stood by the river.

2. And, behold, there came up out of the river seven well favoured kine and fatfleshed; and they fed in a meadow.

3. And, behold, seven other kine came up after them out of the river, ill favoured and leanfleshed; and stood by the other kine upon the brink of the river.

4. And the ill favoured and leanfleshed kine did eat up the seven well favoured and fat kine. So Pharaoh awoke.

5. And he slept and dreamed the second time: and, behold, seven ears of corn came up upon one stalk, rank and good.

6. And, behold, seven thin ears and blasted with the east wind sprung up after them.

7. And the seven thin ears devoured the seven rank and full ears. And Pharaoh awoke, and, behold, it was a dream.

8. And it came to pass in the morning that his spirit was troubled; and he sent and called for all the magicians of Egypt, and all the wise men thereof: and Pharaoh told them his dream; but there was none that could interpret them unto Pharaoh.

**Narrator**

Genesis 41 begins with the words, two full years.

That means Joseph has waited two more years after the cupbearer forgot him.

Two years is not a small pause when you are in prison.

Joseph did the right thing.

Joseph served.

Joseph interpreted correctly.

Joseph asked to be remembered.

And still, two full years pass.

Then Pharaoh dreams.

He stands by the river, likely the Nile.

In Egypt, the Nile was life.

It watered the land.

It fed crops.

It supported cattle, food, trade, and national strength.

So when Pharaoh dreams that something terrible comes up from the river, the dream strikes at Egypt's confidence.

Seven healthy cows are eaten by seven thin cows.

Seven full ears of grain are devoured by seven blasted ears.

The picture is not just strange.

It is threatening.

Something weak and ruined swallows something strong and full.

Pharaoh wakes up troubled.

He calls the magicians and wise men of Egypt, but nobody can interpret the dreams.

That is the setup.

Egypt has power.

Egypt has experts.

Egypt has religion.

Egypt has a throne.

But Egypt does not have the answer.

God is about to bring a forgotten Hebrew prisoner into the room.

## Genesis 41:9-16

**Scripture**

9. Then spake the chief butler unto Pharaoh, saying, I do remember my faults this day:

10. Pharaoh was wroth with his servants, and put me in ward in the captain of the guard's house, both me and the chief baker:

11. And we dreamed a dream in one night, I and he; we dreamed each man according to the interpretation of his dream.

12. And there was there with us a young man, an Hebrew, servant to the captain of the guard; and we told him, and he interpreted to us our dreams; to each man according to his dream he did interpret.

13. And it came to pass, as he interpreted to us, so it was; me he restored unto mine office, and him he hanged.

14. Then Pharaoh sent and called Joseph, and they brought him hastily out of the dungeon: and he shaved himself, and changed his raiment, and came in unto Pharaoh.

15. And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, I have dreamed a dream, and there is none that can interpret it: and I have heard say of thee, that thou canst understand a dream to interpret it.

16. And Joseph answered Pharaoh, saying, It is not in me: God shall give Pharaoh an answer of peace.

**Narrator**

The cupbearer finally remembers.

He says, I remember my faults this day.

That sentence is almost painful.

Joseph asked him to remember two years earlier.

But the cupbearer remembers Joseph only when Pharaoh needs something.

Even so, God uses that delayed memory.

Joseph is described as a young man, a Hebrew, and a servant.

From Pharaoh's court, that is not impressive.

Joseph has no status.

No platform.

No political backing.

No Egyptian credentials.

But he has God.

And that is enough.

Joseph is brought hastily from the dungeon.

After years of slow waiting, the door opens suddenly.

He shaves.

He changes clothes.

He stands before Pharaoh.

And Pharaoh says he has heard Joseph can interpret dreams.

Joseph's first answer tells us what kind of man prison has formed.

He says, It is not in me.

God shall give Pharaoh an answer of peace.

Joseph does not grab glory when the door opens.

He gives God credit immediately.

That is humility without weakness.

Joseph is confident God can answer.

But he is not pretending the power belongs to him.

## Genesis 41:17-32

**Scripture**

17. And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, In my dream, behold, I stood upon the bank of the river:

18. And, behold, there came up out of the river seven kine, fatfleshed and well favoured; and they fed in a meadow:

19. And, behold, seven other kine came up after them, poor and very ill favoured and leanfleshed, such as I never saw in all the land of Egypt for badness:

20. And the lean and the ill favoured kine did eat up the first seven fat kine:

21. And when they had eaten them up, it could not be known that they had eaten them; but they were still ill favoured, as at the beginning. So I awoke.

22. And I saw in my dream, and, behold, seven ears came up in one stalk, full and good:

23. And, behold, seven ears, withered, thin, and blasted with the east wind, sprung up after them:

24. And the thin ears devoured the seven good ears: and I told this unto the magicians; but there was none that could declare it to me.

25. And Joseph said unto Pharaoh, The dream of Pharaoh is one: God hath shewed Pharaoh what he is about to do.

26. The seven good kine are seven years; and the seven good ears are seven years: the dream is one.

27. And the seven thin and ill favoured kine that came up after them are seven years; and the seven empty ears blasted with the east wind shall be seven years of famine.

28. This is the thing which I have spoken unto Pharaoh: What God is about to do he sheweth unto Pharaoh.

29. Behold, there come seven years of great plenty throughout all the land of Egypt:

30. And there shall arise after them seven years of famine; and all the plenty shall be forgotten in the land of Egypt; and the famine shall consume the land;

31. And the plenty shall not be known in the land by reason of that famine following; for it shall be very grievous.

32. And for that the dream was doubled unto Pharaoh twice; it is because the thing is established by God, and God will shortly bring it to pass.

**Narrator**

Pharaoh retells the dream, and he adds a detail that helps us feel it.

The thin cows eat the fat cows, but they still look thin and terrible.

That means the famine will be so severe that the years of plenty will feel forgotten.

Joseph says the dream is one.

Two dreams.

One message.

God is not confusing Pharaoh.

God is confirming the warning.

Seven healthy cows and seven healthy ears of grain mean seven years of plenty.

Seven thin cows and seven blasted ears mean seven years of famine.

Joseph says, God has shown Pharaoh what He is about to do.

This is not Joseph guessing.

This is not Egyptian magic.

This is revelation.

God is showing the future so people can respond wisely before the crisis arrives.

The dream is doubled because the thing is established by God.

In other words, this is not optional.

This is not a maybe.

This is coming.

And because God reveals it beforehand, wisdom has time to prepare.

## Genesis 41:33-45

**Scripture**

33. Now therefore let Pharaoh look out a man discreet and wise, and set him over the land of Egypt.

34. Let Pharaoh do this, and let him appoint officers over the land, and take up the fifth part of the land of Egypt in the seven plenteous years.

35. And let them gather all the food of those good years that come, and lay up corn under the hand of Pharaoh, and let them keep food in the cities.

36. And that food shall be for store to the land against the seven years of famine, which shall be in the land of Egypt; that the land perish not through the famine.

37. And the thing was good in the eyes of Pharaoh, and in the eyes of all his servants.

38. And Pharaoh said unto his servants, Can we find such a one as this is, a man in whom the Spirit of God is?

39. And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, Forasmuch as God hath shewed thee all this, there is none so discreet and wise as thou art:

40. Thou shalt be over my house, and according unto thy word shall all my people be ruled: only in the throne will I be greater than thou.

41. And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, See, I have set thee over all the land of Egypt.

42. And Pharaoh took off his ring from his hand, and put it upon Joseph's hand, and arrayed him in vestures of fine linen, and put a gold chain about his neck;

43. And he made him to ride in the second chariot which he had; and they cried before him, Bow the knee: and he made him ruler over all the land of Egypt.

44. And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, I am Pharaoh, and without thee shall no man lift up his hand or foot in all the land of Egypt.

45. And Pharaoh called Joseph's name Zaphnath-paaneah; and he gave him to wife Asenath the daughter of Poti-pherah priest of On. And Joseph went out over all the land of Egypt.

**Narrator**

Joseph does more than interpret the dream.

He gives a plan.

Find a discreet and wise man.

Appoint officers.

Store one-fifth during the good years.

Keep food in the cities.

Prepare during abundance so the land does not perish during famine.

That is wisdom.

Biblical wisdom is not just knowing what something means.

It is knowing what faithful action should follow.

Pharaoh sees the wisdom and says, Can we find such a one as this is, a man in whom the Spirit of God is?

That is a striking sentence.

An Egyptian ruler recognizes that something divine is at work in Joseph.

Then Joseph is promoted.

And the reversal is huge.

Joseph begins the day in prison.

He ends the day over Egypt.

Pharaoh gives him the ring from his hand.

That means delegated authority.

He gives him fine linen.

That means honor.

He gives him a gold chain.

That means public status.

He makes him ride in the second chariot.

People bow before him.

And do not miss the clothing detail.

Joseph's brothers stripped his robe.

Potiphar's wife kept his garment.

Now Pharaoh clothes him with honor.

The garment story is being reversed.

The one who was stripped is now clothed.

The one who was powerless is now entrusted with power.

## Genesis 41:46-57

**Scripture**

46. And Joseph was thirty years old when he stood before Pharaoh king of Egypt. And Joseph went out from the presence of Pharaoh, and went throughout all the land of Egypt.

47. And in the seven plenteous years the earth brought forth by handfuls.

48. And he gathered up all the food of the seven years, which were in the land of Egypt, and laid up the food in the cities: the food of the field, which was round about every city, laid he up in the same.

49. And Joseph gathered corn as the sand of the sea, very much, until he left numbering; for it was without number.

50. And unto Joseph were born two sons before the years of famine came, which Asenath the daughter of Poti-pherah priest of On bare unto him.

51. And Joseph called the name of the firstborn Manasseh: For God, said he, hath made me forget all my toil, and all my father's house.

52. And the name of the second called he Ephraim: For God hath caused me to be fruitful in the land of my affliction.

53. And the seven years of plenteousness, that was in the land of Egypt, were ended.

54. And the seven years of dearth began to come, according as Joseph had said: and the dearth was in all lands; but in all the land of Egypt there was bread.

55. And when all the land of Egypt was famished, the people cried to Pharaoh for bread: and Pharaoh said unto all the Egyptians, Go unto Joseph; what he saith to you, do.

56. And the famine was over all the face of the earth: and Joseph opened all the storehouses, and sold unto the Egyptians; and the famine waxed sore in the land of Egypt.

57. And all countries came into Egypt to Joseph for to buy corn; because that the famine was so sore in all lands.

**Narrator**

Joseph is thirty years old when he stands before Pharaoh.

He was seventeen when his brothers sold him.

That means about thirteen years have passed.

Thirteen years of betrayal, slavery, temptation, false accusation, prison, service, and waiting.

But those years were not wasted.

Joseph now has wisdom, endurance, administrative experience, humility, and the fear of God.

During the years of plenty, Joseph stores grain until it cannot be numbered.

Then Joseph has two sons.

Their names are important.

The first is Manasseh.

Joseph says God has made him forget all his toil and all his father's house.

That does not mean Joseph has no memory.

Genesis 42 will show he remembers very clearly.

It means God has given him relief from the crushing weight of the pain.

The second is Ephraim.

Joseph says God has made him fruitful in the land of his affliction.

That phrase is beautiful.

Egypt is still the land of affliction.

Joseph does not pretend the painful place was not painful.

But God made him fruitful there.

Then the famine comes, just as Joseph said.

But in Egypt there is bread.

People cry to Pharaoh, and Pharaoh says, Go to Joseph.

Joseph opens the storehouses.

And all countries begin coming to Egypt for grain.

That includes Joseph's family.

## Genesis 42:1-9

**Scripture**

1. Now when Jacob saw that there was corn in Egypt, Jacob said unto his sons, Why do ye look one upon another?

2. And he said, Behold, I have heard that there is corn in Egypt: get you down thither, and buy for us from thence; that we may live, and not die.

3. And Joseph's ten brethren went down to buy corn in Egypt.

4. But Benjamin, Joseph's brother, Jacob sent not with his brethren; for he said, Lest peradventure mischief befall him.

5. And the sons of Israel came to buy corn among those that came: for the famine was in the land of Canaan.

6. And Joseph was the governor over the land, and he it was that sold to all the people of the land: and Joseph's brethren came, and bowed down themselves before him with their faces to the earth.

7. And Joseph saw his brethren, and he knew them, but made himself strange unto them, and spake roughly unto them; and he said unto them, Whence come ye? And they said, From the land of Canaan to buy food.

8. And Joseph knew his brethren, but they knew not him.

9. And Joseph remembered the dreams which he dreamed of them, and said unto them, Ye are spies; to see the nakedness of the land ye are come.

**Narrator**

Now the famine reaches Canaan.

That matters because Canaan is where Jacob's family lives.

This is the covenant family.

These are the descendants of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

And they need grain from Egypt.

Jacob sends Joseph's ten brothers, but he keeps Benjamin home.

Benjamin is Joseph's full brother, Rachel's other son.

Jacob believes Joseph is dead, so he is terrified of losing Benjamin too.

Then the brothers come before Joseph.

And they bow with their faces to the earth.

This is Genesis 37 coming back into the room.

Joseph's dreams are beginning to happen in real life.

The brothers do not recognize Joseph.

Years have passed.

Joseph is older.

He is dressed like an Egyptian ruler.

He speaks through an interpreter.

He holds authority they never imagined.

But Joseph recognizes them.

And verse 9 says Joseph remembered the dreams.

That does not mean Joseph becomes petty.

It means he realizes God has carried the story through every place Joseph could not control.

The dreams were not dead.

God's word was not dead.

## Genesis 42:10-24

**Scripture**

10. And they said unto him, Nay, my lord, but to buy food are thy servants come.

11. We are all one man's sons; we are true men, thy servants are no spies.

12. And he said unto them, Nay, but to see the nakedness of the land ye are come.

13. And they said, Thy servants are twelve brethren, the sons of one man in the land of Canaan; and, behold, the youngest is this day with our father, and one is not.

14. And Joseph said unto them, That is it that I spake unto you, saying, Ye are spies:

15. Hereby ye shall be proved: By the life of Pharaoh ye shall not go forth hence, except your youngest brother come hither.

16. Send one of you, and let him fetch your brother, and ye shall be kept in prison, that your words may be proved, whether there be any truth in you: or else by the life of Pharaoh surely ye are spies.

17. And he put them all together into ward three days.

18. And Joseph said unto them the third day, This do, and live; for I fear God:

19. If ye be true men, let one of your brethren be bound in the house of your prison: go ye, carry corn for the famine of your houses:

20. But bring your youngest brother unto me; so shall your words be verified, and ye shall not die. And they did so.

21. And they said one to another, We are verily guilty concerning our brother, in that we saw the anguish of his soul, when he besought us, and we would not hear; therefore is this distress come upon us.

22. And Reuben answered them, saying, Spake I not unto you, saying, Do not sin against the child; and ye would not hear? therefore, behold, also his blood is required.

23. And they knew not that Joseph understood them; for he spake unto them by an interpreter.

24. And he turned himself about from them, and wept; and returned to them again, and communed with them, and took from them Simeon, and bound him before their eyes.

**Narrator**

Joseph accuses them of being spies.

At first that can sound harsh.

But Joseph is not simply gathering information.

He is testing them.

Are these men still the same men who sold him?

Will they abandon another brother?

Will they tell the truth?

Will they care about their father?

Their own words expose the family story.

They say they are twelve brothers.

The youngest is with their father.

And one is not.

They are talking about Joseph while Joseph is listening.

They think he is gone.

They think he is not.

But he is standing right in front of them.

Joseph puts them in prison for three days.

The brothers who once threw Joseph into a pit now taste confinement and fear.

Then on the third day, Joseph says, This do, and live; for I fear God.

That line matters.

Joseph is testing them, but he is not acting without moral boundaries.

Then their guilt finally speaks out loud.

They say, We are truly guilty concerning our brother.

And then Genesis gives us a detail we did not hear back in chapter 37.

Joseph begged them.

They saw the anguish of his soul.

He pleaded.

And they would not listen.

That makes the old betrayal heavier.

Joseph did not go silently into the pit and sale.

He cried out.

And his brothers ignored him.

Joseph hears all of this.

They do not know he understands.

And Joseph turns away and weeps.

That is important.

This is not cold revenge.

This is painful.

Joseph is powerful now, but he is still wounded.

## Genesis 42:25-38

**Scripture**

25. Then Joseph commanded to fill their sacks with corn, and to restore every man's money into his sack, and to give them provision for the way: and thus did he unto them.

26. And they laded their asses with the corn, and departed thence.

27. And as one of them opened his sack to give his ass provender in the inn, he espied his money; for, behold, it was in his sack's mouth.

28. And he said unto his brethren, My money is restored; and, lo, it is even in my sack: and their heart failed them, and they were afraid, saying one to another, What is this that God hath done unto us?

29. And they came unto Jacob their father unto the land of Canaan, and told him all that befell unto them; saying,

30. The man, who is the lord of the land, spake roughly to us, and took us for spies of the country.

31. And we said unto him, We are true men; we are no spies:

32. We be twelve brethren, sons of our father; one is not, and the youngest is this day with our father in the land of Canaan.

33. And the man, the lord of the country, said unto us, Hereby shall I know that ye are true men; leave one of your brethren here with me, and take food for the famine of your households, and be gone:

34. And bring your youngest brother unto me: then shall I know that ye are no spies, but that ye are true men: so will I deliver you your brother, and ye shall traffick in the land.

35. And it came to pass as they emptied their sacks, that, behold, every man's bundle of money was in his sack: and when both they and their father saw the bundles of money, they were afraid.

36. And Jacob their father said unto them, Me have ye bereaved of my children: Joseph is not, and Simeon is not, and ye will take Benjamin away: all these things are against me.

37. And Reuben spake unto his father, saying, Slay my two sons, if I bring him not to thee: deliver him into my hand, and I will bring him to thee again.

38. And he said, My son shall not go down with you; for his brother is dead, and he is left alone: if mischief befall him by the way in the which ye go, then shall ye bring down my gray hairs with sorrow to the grave.

**Narrator**

Joseph fills their sacks with grain.

He restores their money.

He gives them provision for the journey.

That is mercy.

But the brothers cannot read it as mercy.

When they find the money, their hearts fail.

They are afraid.

They say, What is this that God has done to us?

That sentence tells us what guilt does.

Guilt makes even kindness feel like judgment.

They return to Jacob and explain what happened.

Simeon is left behind.

Benjamin must go to Egypt.

And Jacob says, Joseph is not, and Simeon is not, and you will take Benjamin away.

All these things are against me.

From Jacob's angle, it looks like everything is loss.

But the reader knows more than Jacob knows.

Joseph is alive.

Simeon is alive.

The grain is real.

The money has been returned.

God is moving the family toward truth.

But Jacob cannot see that yet.

Reuben offers his sons as a guarantee, but Jacob refuses.

Benjamin will not go.

The family is not ready yet.

The test will continue.

## Closing

Day 17 is a major turning point.

Joseph begins forgotten in prison.

Then Pharaoh dreams.

The cupbearer remembers.

Joseph is brought out.

Joseph gives God the glory.

Joseph interprets the dreams.

Joseph offers wise action.

Joseph is raised over Egypt.

But the story does not stop with Joseph's promotion.

God is not only lifting Joseph.

God is preserving life.

And God is bringing Joseph's brothers toward truth.

That is why Genesis 42 matters so much.

The brothers bow.

Joseph remembers.

Their guilt speaks.

Joseph weeps.

And Jacob still thinks everything is against him.

But it is not.

God is doing more than Jacob can see.

If you carry one thing from Genesis 41 and 42, carry this:

God can be working in the years that feel forgotten, and He can use pressure to bring truth into the light.

Day 18 will continue the test, especially around Benjamin and Judah.

But Day 17 shows us the first movement:

Joseph rises.

The brothers bow.

And the hidden wound begins to open.
