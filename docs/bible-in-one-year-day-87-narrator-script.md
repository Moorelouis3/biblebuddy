# Day 87 Narrator Script

## Opening

Welcome to Day 87 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading First Kings 20 through 22 and Second Kings 1.

The title for today is Ahab's Fall and Elijah's Final Warnings.

This day shows the mercy and judgment of God around Ahab's house.

Ahab receives victories he does not deserve.

He spares Ben-hadad when God had appointed judgment.

He desires Naboth's vineyard and lets Jezebel murder an innocent man to get it.

Micaiah tells the truth when hundreds of prophets tell Ahab what he wants to hear.

Ahab dies in battle just as the word of the LORD said.

Then Second Kings opens with Ahaziah, Ahab's son, seeking Baalzebub instead of the LORD.

The story is dark, but it is not confusing.

The LORD keeps speaking.

The question is whether the kings will listen.

## First Kings 20

First Kings 20 begins with Ben-hadad king of Syria coming against Samaria with a great host.

He demands Ahab's silver, gold, wives, and children.

Ahab first yields, but Ben-hadad presses further and threatens to search his house and servants' houses.

The elders of Israel tell Ahab not to consent.

Ben-hadad boasts, but Ahab answers with a proverb: Let not him that girdeth on his harness boast himself as he that putteth it off.

Then a prophet comes to Ahab.

This is surprising mercy.

Ahab is wicked, yet the LORD sends a word promising deliverance so Ahab will know that He is the LORD.

The young men of the princes of the provinces go out first.

Israel defeats Syria.

The prophet warns Ahab that Syria will return.

The Syrians think Israel's God is a God of the hills and not of the valleys.

That is theology from pagans trying to explain defeat.

The LORD answers that false idea by giving Israel victory in the valley too.

He is not a local hill god.

He is the LORD over all.

After the victory, Ben-hadad's servants come with sackcloth and ropes, asking for mercy.

Ahab calls Ben-hadad his brother and makes a covenant with him.

But a prophet later rebukes Ahab with a acted parable.

Because Ahab let go the man whom God appointed to utter destruction, Ahab's life will go for Ben-hadad's life and his people for Ben-hadad's people.

Ahab goes home heavy and displeased.

He has received mercy, but he still resists the word of the LORD.

## First Kings 21

First Kings 21 tells the story of Naboth's vineyard.

Naboth has a vineyard near Ahab's palace.

Ahab wants it for a garden of herbs.

Naboth refuses because it is the inheritance of his fathers.

This is not stubbornness over a random field.

Land inheritance in Israel is tied to family, covenant, and God's gift.

Ahab goes home heavy and displeased, lays on his bed, turns away his face, and refuses to eat.

Jezebel asks if he now governs Israel.

She writes letters in Ahab's name, seals them with his seal, proclaims a fast, sets Naboth on high, and arranges false witnesses.

Naboth is accused of blaspheming God and the king.

He is stoned and dies.

Then Jezebel tells Ahab to arise and take possession.

The king gets the vineyard through murder.

But the word of the LORD comes to Elijah.

Elijah confronts Ahab with the words, Hast thou killed, and also taken possession?

The judgment is severe.

Dogs will lick Ahab's blood where they licked Naboth's blood.

Judgment will fall on Ahab's house.

Jezebel also will be eaten by dogs by the wall of Jezreel.

The chapter says there was none like Ahab, who sold himself to work wickedness, whom Jezebel his wife stirred up.

Yet when Ahab hears the words, he humbles himself.

The LORD notices.

Judgment on his house will not come in his days, but in his son's days.

This is a serious mercy.

It does not erase Ahab's guilt, but it shows that the LORD sees even humbled response.

## First Kings 22

First Kings 22 brings Ahab to his final battle.

Jehoshaphat of Judah joins Ahab of Israel.

Ahab wants to take Ramoth-gilead from Syria.

Jehoshaphat asks for the word of the LORD.

About four hundred prophets tell Ahab to go up, because the Lord will deliver it into the king's hand.

But Jehoshaphat asks if there is not a prophet of the LORD besides.

Ahab says there is Micaiah, but he hates him because he does not prophesy good concerning him, but evil.

That sentence reveals Ahab's heart.

He does not want truth.

He wants favorable words.

The messenger pressures Micaiah to speak like the other prophets.

Micaiah says he will speak what the LORD says.

At first he answers with irony.

Then he gives the true vision: Israel scattered upon the hills as sheep without a shepherd.

He also says he saw the LORD on His throne, and a lying spirit would entice Ahab's prophets.

Ahab rejects the warning and imprisons Micaiah.

He goes into battle disguised.

But disguise cannot hide him from God's word.

A certain man draws a bow at a venture and strikes the king of Israel between the joints of the harness.

Ahab dies in his chariot.

The dogs lick his blood, according to the word of the LORD.

The chapter also summarizes Jehoshaphat, who does right like Asa though the high places remain, and Ahaziah, who walks in the way of Ahab and serves Baal.

## Second Kings 1

Second Kings opens with Moab rebelling after Ahab's death.

Ahaziah falls through a lattice and becomes sick.

Instead of seeking the LORD, he sends messengers to inquire of Baalzebub the god of Ekron.

Elijah meets them with the word of the LORD.

Is it not because there is not a God in Israel that you go to inquire of Baalzebub?

Because of this, Ahaziah will not come down from his bed, but will surely die.

The king sends captains with fifties to seize Elijah.

Fire comes down from heaven and consumes the first two captains and their fifties.

The third captain humbles himself and pleads for his life.

The angel of the LORD tells Elijah to go down with him.

Elijah tells Ahaziah the same message face to face.

Ahaziah dies according to the word of the LORD.

The chapter shows that Ahab's house has not learned.

The throne changed hands, but idolatry remained.

## Full Lesson Review

Day 87 shows repeated warnings and rejected truth.

Ahab receives victory by mercy but disobeys after victory.

Ahab takes Naboth's vineyard through injustice and murder.

Micaiah speaks the word of the LORD, but Ahab chooses flattering voices.

Ahab dies exactly as the word of the LORD said.

Ahaziah seeks Baalzebub instead of the LORD and dies under prophetic judgment.

The day teaches that God's patience is real, but His warnings are not empty.

When the LORD speaks, kings are accountable.

## Slow Walk Through the Day

Day 87 should be heard as a series of chances to listen.

Ahab is not destroyed the first time the reader sees him fail.

The LORD gives him victories over Syria so he may know that the LORD is God.

That mercy is important.

God is not treating Ahab as if he never heard.

The king receives evidence, rescue, warning, and prophetic speech.

But Ahab keeps bending away from obedience.

In First Kings 20, the issue is not only military strategy.

The Syrians misunderstand God.

They think Israel's God is limited to the hills.

The LORD answers in the valley so His name is known.

Ahab should have learned worship, humility, and obedience from that deliverance.

Instead, he makes his own decision with Ben-hadad and ignores the weight of God's command.

In First Kings 21, Ahab's private desire becomes public injustice.

Naboth's vineyard exposes the king's heart.

Naboth understands inheritance as something received under God.

Ahab treats it as something he should get because he wants it.

Jezebel then uses law language, fasting, witnesses, and public accusation to commit murder.

That is one of the ugliest parts of the story.

Religious form is being used to cover injustice.

Elijah's question cuts through everything: Hast thou killed, and also taken possession?

The LORD sees both the murder and the greed behind it.

In First Kings 22, the issue becomes truth.

Ahab can gather hundreds of agreeable voices.

He can dress falsehood in confidence.

He can imprison the prophet who tells the truth.

But he cannot make the word of the LORD fail.

Micaiah's vision shows that heaven is the real court above the royal courts of earth.

Ahab disguises himself, but a random arrow still finds him.

The word of the LORD is more accurate than human strategy.

Second Kings 1 then shows Ahab's son repeating the family pattern.

Ahaziah seeks Baalzebub instead of the LORD.

The question from Elijah is simple and devastating.

Is there not a God in Israel?

The problem is not lack of access.

The problem is misplaced trust.

## Prayerful Landing

Day 87 warns the listener about wanting comfortable words more than true words.

Ahab hates Micaiah because Micaiah does not prophesy good concerning him.

That is dangerous.

Truth does not become evil because it confronts us.

Mercy does not disappear because God warns us.

The faithful heart must learn to receive the word of the LORD even when it exposes desire, pride, injustice, or fear.

Naboth's vineyard teaches that God sees hidden injustice.

Micaiah teaches that one truthful voice is worth more than hundreds of flattering voices.

Ahaziah teaches that seeking help apart from the LORD is still rebellion, even when life is painful.

This day calls the listener to stop managing appearances and start listening.

The LORD's word is not background commentary.

It is the truth by which kings live or die.

## Final Guided Recap

Before the closing, gather the warnings of the day.

First Kings 20 shows mercy before judgment.

Ahab is not helped because he is righteous.

He is helped so he will know that the LORD is God.

The victories over Syria should have led to humility.

Instead, Ahab chooses his own way with Ben-hadad and leaves the chapter heavy and displeased.

First Kings 21 shows desire becoming oppression.

Ahab wants a vineyard.

Naboth refuses because inheritance is not just property.

Jezebel uses Ahab's name, royal seal, false witnesses, and public religion to murder an innocent man.

Elijah's confrontation shows that God sees what power tries to hide.

First Kings 22 shows the danger of preferring agreement over truth.

The four hundred prophets create confidence, but Micaiah speaks reality.

Ahab can disguise himself, but he cannot escape the word of the LORD.

The arrow that seems random is not random to God.

Second Kings 1 shows the next generation repeating the same problem.

Ahaziah does not seek the LORD.

He seeks Baalzebub.

Elijah's message is direct: the king will die because he looked away from the God of Israel.

The day is heavy because every chapter shows the same root problem.

The LORD speaks, but the kings keep resisting.

The listener should leave with holy seriousness.

Truth is mercy when God sends it.

Ignoring that truth is destruction.

## Closing

As you finish Day 87, remember Micaiah's courage.

Many voices can agree and still be wrong.

The faithful question is not, What do I want to hear?

The faithful question is, What has the LORD spoken?
