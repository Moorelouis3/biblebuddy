# Day 100 Narrator Script

## Opening

Welcome to Day 100 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading First Chronicles 25 through 28.

The title for today is Worship Teams and Temple Plans.

This day continues David's preparation for the temple.

Musicians are organized.

Porters are assigned.

Treasurers and officers are named.

Military divisions and tribal leaders are listed.

Then David gathers Israel's leaders and charges Solomon to build the house of the LORD.

Day 100 teaches that worship and leadership require ordered service, faithful people, and courage rooted in God's command.

## First Chronicles 25

First Chronicles 25 organizes the singers.

David and the captains separate sons of Asaph, Heman, and Jeduthun for the service of song.

They prophesy with harps, psalteries, and cymbals.

This shows that music in temple worship is not entertainment added on the side.

It is holy service before the LORD.

The chapter names skilled singers and their courses by lot.

Small and great, teacher and scholar, are ordered together.

That detail matters.

Worship involves skill, training, humility, and assigned service.

The sound of praise is organized for the house of God.

## First Chronicles 26

First Chronicles 26 turns to porters, treasurers, and officers.

The porters guard gates.

Their work may seem ordinary, but gates matter.

They protect access to holy space and support the ordered life of worship.

The chapter also names those over treasures of the house of God and dedicated things.

Some treasures came from victories and were dedicated for temple service.

Other Levites are assigned outward business over Israel as officers and judges.

The chapter shows that worship life includes guarding, accounting, judging, and stewarding.

Faithfulness is not only visible singing.

It also includes trustworthy service in places many people may not notice.

## First Chronicles 27

First Chronicles 27 lists military divisions, tribal rulers, and stewards over the king's substance.

There are monthly courses for service.

There are leaders over tribes.

There are workers over treasures, storehouses, fields, vineyards, olive trees, cattle, camels, flocks, and sons.

David's kingdom is ordered in detail.

The chapter reminds the listener that a kingdom requires administration.

Food, fields, animals, armies, records, and counsel all matter.

But Chronicles also notes that David did not count those under twenty because the LORD had said He would increase Israel like the stars of heaven.

That keeps the story tied to God's promise.

Human organization must stay under divine promise.

## First Chronicles 28

First Chronicles 28 brings the leaders of Israel together.

David tells them that he had it in his heart to build a house of rest for the ark of the covenant and the footstool of God.

But God said David would not build because he had been a man of war.

God chose Solomon to sit on the throne and build the house.

David charges Solomon in front of Israel.

Know thou the God of thy father, and serve Him with a perfect heart and willing mind.

If Solomon seeks Him, He will be found.

If he forsakes Him, He will cast him off forever.

Then David gives Solomon the pattern for the temple, porch, houses, treasuries, chambers, courts, courses, vessels, altar, and chariot of the cherubims.

David says the LORD made him understand in writing by His hand upon him.

He tells Solomon to be strong and of good courage, and do it.

The LORD God will be with him.

## Full Lesson Review

Day 100 is about ordered preparation.

Singers are appointed.

Gatekeepers guard.

Treasurers steward.

Officers judge.

Military and tribal leaders serve.

David gives Solomon the temple pattern and charges him to know and serve the LORD.

The day teaches that temple worship is not random enthusiasm.

It is a whole life of service ordered before God.

## Slow Walk

Do not overlook the ordinary roles.

Porters, treasurers, singers, officers, stewards, and captains may not all stand in the same spotlight, but they all serve the larger purpose.

The temple will need more than stones and gold.

It will need faithful people.

David's charge to Solomon is the heart of the day.

Know God.

Serve Him with a perfect heart.

Seek Him.

Be strong.

Do the work.

The plans matter, but Solomon's heart matters more.

The house of God must be built by a king who knows the God of the house.

## Deeper Teaching

Day 100 can feel administrative, but it is actually deeply spiritual.

First Chronicles 25 shows musicians separated for service.

They are not simply performers.

They prophesy with instruments.

Their music belongs to the worship life of the temple.

The mention of teacher and scholar shows training.

The mention of lots shows order.

The mention of families shows continuity.

Praise is treated as holy work.

That matters for BibleBuddy listeners because worship is not only spontaneous feeling.

It can include skill, discipline, training, and assigned responsibility.

First Chronicles 26 shows porters and treasurers.

Gatekeepers may seem less exciting than singers, but their work is essential.

They guard access.

They protect holy order.

They serve where many people may never notice.

Treasurers also matter because dedicated things must be handled faithfully.

Gold, silver, vessels, and spoil from victories are not private prizes.

They belong to the service of the LORD.

First Chronicles 27 broadens the kingdom administration.

Monthly military divisions, tribal rulers, storehouse keepers, vineyard keepers, olive keepers, cattle keepers, camel keepers, flock keepers, counselors, and scribes all appear.

The kingdom is not only worship services.

It is organized life under God's promise.

Fields, animals, armies, counsel, and records must be stewarded.

First Chronicles 28 brings the heart of the day.

David gathers the leaders and charges Solomon.

He explains that he wanted to build, but God chose Solomon.

Then he says, Know thou the God of thy father.

That comes before building.

Serve Him with a perfect heart and willing mind.

That comes before architecture.

Seek Him.

That comes before success.

David gives Solomon plans, but he also gives him a spiritual warning.

If Solomon seeks the LORD, He will be found.

If Solomon forsakes Him, he will be cast off.

The temple plan matters because it came by God's hand, but the builder's heart matters too.

God's house must not be built by a heart that does not seek God.

## Extended Narration

Day 100 continues the preparation for temple worship, and it shows that worship involves more than one kind of servant.

First Chronicles 25 focuses on singers and musicians.

That may surprise a reader who expects temple preparation to be only about builders, priests, and sacrifices.

But Chronicles gives serious attention to praise.

The singers are not treated as decoration.

They are appointed for service.

They prophesy with harps, psalteries, and cymbals.

Their music belongs to the ordered worship of God.

This teaches that praise is not just emotional background.

Praise is ministry.

Praise teaches the people what is true.

Praise helps the congregation remember the LORD.

Praise gives words and sound to thanksgiving, reverence, and hope.

Then chapter 26 speaks about porters, gatekeepers, treasurers, and officers.

These roles may sound less exciting, but Chronicles does not treat them as unimportant.

The gatekeepers guard access.

The treasurers care for dedicated things.

The officers and judges help the kingdom live with order.

The temple community needs visible servants and hidden servants.

It needs songs and safeguards.

It needs public praise and careful stewardship.

This is a major lesson for anyone learning the Bible.

God's work is not carried by one impressive person.

It is carried through many faithful servants doing different assigned tasks before the LORD.

Chapter 27 then widens the scene to the kingdom's order.

There are captains by courses, princes over tribes, storehouse keepers, vineyard keepers, shepherds, counselors, and companions of the king.

Chronicles is showing a whole life ordered under God's covenant.

The temple is central, but the kingdom around it also needs faithful stewardship.

Fields matter.

Storehouses matter.

Vineyards matter.

Counsel matters.

Leadership matters.

The life of God's people is not split into holy moments and meaningless ordinary work.

Ordinary responsibilities can become service when they are carried before God with faithfulness.

Then chapter 28 brings David's public charge.

David gathers the leaders and explains that Solomon will build the temple.

He reminds them that God chose Judah, David's house, David himself, and Solomon.

The language of choosing is important.

The temple project is not Solomon's personal dream.

It rests on God's decision.

David then speaks directly to Solomon.

Know thou the God of thy father.

Serve him with a perfect heart and with a willing mind.

That line may be the spiritual center of the day.

Solomon needs plans, workers, gold, silver, and skill.

But before all of that, Solomon needs to know God.

He needs a heart that is whole before God.

He needs a mind that serves willingly.

David warns him that the LORD searches all hearts and understands all imaginations of the thoughts.

That means the temple cannot be built as an outward performance hiding an inward rebellion.

God sees the builder.

God sees the motive.

God sees the heart.

David gives Solomon the pattern for the temple and says the hand of the LORD was upon him in understanding.

So the plans are not treated as random human creativity.

They are received as part of God's direction for His house.

For a beginner, Day 100 teaches that the house of God is prepared through ordered worship, faithful service, careful stewardship, and a heart that truly seeks the LORD.

The singers matter.

The gatekeepers matter.

The treasurers matter.

The leaders matter.

Solomon matters.

But above all, the LORD matters.

The temple will be beautiful, but beauty is not enough.

The service will be organized, but organization is not enough.

The music will be skillful, but skill is not enough.

God calls His people to serve Him with a whole heart.
