# Bible in One Year - Day 37 Narrator Script

**Reading:** Leviticus 21-24

## Opening

Welcome to Day 37 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Leviticus chapters 21 through 24.

The theme is priests, feasts, and sacred order.

Leviticus has been teaching us that God is holy, and that His people are called to live near Him with reverence.

Today that holiness touches priests, offerings, the yearly feasts, the lamp in the sanctuary, the bread before the LORD, and justice in the camp.

## Leviticus 21 verses 1 through 24

**Scripture**

Leviticus 21 gives standards for the priests.

The priests must be careful around death, mourning, marriage, family honor, and altar service.

The high priest has even stricter instructions, because he carries the anointing oil and serves in a special way before the LORD.

The chapter also teaches that a priest with a blemish may eat the holy food, but he may not approach to offer the bread of God.

**Narrator**

This chapter can feel strange at first.

But the main idea is not that priests are better people.

The main idea is that holy service must visibly point to the holiness of God.

Priests stand close to the altar.

They handle holy things.

They teach Israel what it means to draw near to the LORD.

So their lives, homes, grief, bodies, and public service all carry meaning.

Even this chapter's hard details are teaching one simple truth.

Nearness to God is not casual.

## Leviticus 22 verses 1 through 16

**Scripture**

Leviticus 22 continues with the priests and the holy things of the children of Israel.

If a priest is unclean, he must not eat of the holy things until he is clean.

The chapter names who may eat the holy food and who may not.

It also warns the priests not to profane the holy things that the people offer unto the LORD.

**Narrator**

Holy food is food connected to worship.

It is not ordinary leftovers.

It belongs to the service of the LORD.

That is why the priests must handle it carefully.

For a beginner, this helps us understand Leviticus better.

The book is not only about avoiding wrong behavior.

It is about learning the difference between common and holy.

God is training Israel to treat His presence, His altar, His gifts, and His name with reverence.

## Leviticus 22 verses 17 through 33

**Scripture**

The LORD tells Moses to speak to Aaron, his sons, and all the children of Israel.

Offerings brought to the LORD must be without blemish.

A blind, broken, maimed, diseased, or damaged animal must not be offered.

The chapter closes with the LORD saying that He brought Israel out of Egypt to be their God.

**Narrator**

Without blemish means whole and sound.

Israel must not bring God what is careless, corrupt, or damaged.

The offering teaches the worshiper something.

God is holy.

God is worthy.

God should not receive what people would be ashamed to give anywhere else.

This also connects backward to Egypt.

The LORD rescued Israel, not so they could worship however they wanted, but so they could belong to Him as His holy people.

## Leviticus 23 verses 1 through 14

**Scripture**

Leviticus 23 begins the feasts of the LORD.

The Sabbath comes first as a weekly holy convocation.

Then the chapter speaks of Passover, Unleavened Bread, and Firstfruits.

Israel must remember the LORD's rescue and honor Him at the beginning of harvest.

**Narrator**

This chapter teaches that God owns time.

Israel's calendar is not just work, harvest, travel, and survival.

Their year is shaped by worship.

Sabbath teaches rest before God.

Passover remembers rescue from Egypt.

Unleavened Bread keeps the memory of deliverance alive.

Firstfruits teaches Israel to honor God before enjoying the fullness of the harvest.

God is forming a people who remember Him with their time, not only with their sacrifices.

## Leviticus 23 verses 15 through 44

**Scripture**

The feasts continue.

Israel counts seven Sabbaths to the feast of Weeks.

They leave gleanings for the poor and the stranger.

They observe a memorial of blowing trumpets.

They keep the Day of Atonement.

They rejoice in the feast of Tabernacles and remember how the LORD made Israel dwell in booths when He brought them out of Egypt.

**Narrator**

Holy time stretches through the whole year.

Weeks celebrates harvest.

Trumpets gathers the people with a holy alarm.

The Day of Atonement calls the nation to humility before God.

Tabernacles teaches joy and memory.

The booth is important.

It reminds Israel that they were once travelers who depended completely on the LORD.

So the feast is not only celebration.

It is worshipful memory.

God does not want His people to forget where they came from or who carried them.

## Leviticus 24 verses 1 through 9

**Scripture**

The LORD commands pure olive oil for the lamp, so it may burn continually before Him.

Aaron must order it from evening to morning before the LORD.

Then twelve cakes of bread are placed in two rows on the pure table before the LORD.

This bread is for Aaron and his sons in a holy place.

**Narrator**

The lamp and the bread are sanctuary signs.

The lamp gives continual light before the LORD.

The bread is set in order before Him.

There are twelve loaves, which points to the twelve tribes of Israel.

This is not random decoration.

It is worship with meaning.

The light keeps shining.

The bread remains before the LORD.

Israel is being taught that the life of the people is continually held before God's presence.

## Leviticus 24 verses 10 through 23

**Scripture**

A man blasphemes the name of the LORD and curses.

The people put him in ward until the mind of the LORD is shown to them.

The LORD commands judgment.

The chapter also gives justice language about injury, life, and equal judgment for the stranger and for one born in the land.

**Narrator**

This section moves from the sanctuary to the camp.

The holy name of the LORD must not be cursed or treated as common.

God's name reveals who He is.

To attack His name is not a small thing in Israel.

The justice laws also matter.

They teach that punishment must be measured and not chaotic.

The same law applies to the stranger and the Israelite.

Holiness is not only about worship rituals.

It also shapes speech, judgment, and public life.

## Closing

That is Day 37.

Leviticus 21 through 24 shows us priests, offerings, feasts, lamps, bread, speech, and justice under the holiness of God.

The big lesson is simple.

When the LORD lives among His people, nothing is ordinary in the same way anymore.

Worship matters.

Time matters.

Words matter.

Justice matters.

God is forming a people who learn to live every part of life before Him.
