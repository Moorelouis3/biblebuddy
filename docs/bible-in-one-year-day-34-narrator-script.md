# Bible In One Year - Day 34 Narrator Script

## Day 34 - Worship, Holiness, and Clean Living

**Reading:** Leviticus 9 through 12
**Bible text:** King James Version, Leviticus 9-12
**Audio style:** One narrator, cinematic Bible teaching with Scripture reading

## Opening

Welcome to Day 34 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Leviticus 9 through 12.

Yesterday Aaron and his sons were washed, clothed, anointed, and marked with blood.

They were consecrated for priestly service.

Today, that priestly service begins.

Leviticus 9 is beautiful.

Aaron offers sacrifices.

Moses and Aaron bless the people.

The glory of the LORD appears.

Fire comes out from before the LORD and consumes the offering.

The people shout and fall on their faces.

But then Leviticus 10 is shocking.

Nadab and Abihu offer strange fire before the LORD.

Fire comes out again.

This time it brings judgment.

Then Leviticus 11 and 12 begin teaching Israel about clean and unclean in daily life.

Animals.

Food.

Dead bodies.

Childbirth.

Washing.

Waiting.

Purification.

The movement matters.

God's glory is near.

Priests must serve carefully.

And Israel must learn that holiness touches ordinary life.

## Leviticus 9:1-24

Leviticus 9 verses 1 through 24.

**Scripture**

And it came to pass on the eighth day, that Moses called Aaron and his sons, and the elders of Israel.

And he said unto Aaron, Take thee a young calf for a sin offering, and a ram for a burnt offering, without blemish, and offer them before the LORD.

And unto the children of Israel thou shalt speak, saying, Take ye a kid of the goats for a sin offering; and a calf and a lamb, both of the first year, without blemish, for a burnt offering.

And Moses said, This is the thing which the LORD commanded that ye should do: and the glory of the LORD shall appear unto you.

And Aaron lifted up his hand toward the people, and blessed them.

And Moses and Aaron went into the tabernacle of the congregation, and came out, and blessed the people.

And the glory of the LORD appeared unto all the people.

And there came a fire out from before the LORD, and consumed upon the altar the burnt offering and the fat.

Which when all the people saw, they shouted, and fell on their faces.

**Narrator**

Leviticus 9 begins on the eighth day.

That means the seven days of ordination are complete.

Aaron now begins public priestly ministry.

But notice the order.

Aaron offers for himself first.

The priest who serves the people also needs atonement.

He does not stand above mercy.

He stands under it.

Then Aaron offers for the people.

The sacrifices are made as the LORD commanded.

And then the glory of the LORD appears.

Fire comes out from before the LORD and consumes the offering.

This is not a performance trick.

It is God's visible acceptance of worship offered His way.

The people shout.

They fall on their faces.

Their joy and fear belong together.

God is near.

God has accepted the offering.

And holy worship has become visible before the whole congregation.

## Leviticus 10:1-7

Leviticus 10 verses 1 through 7.

**Scripture**

And Nadab and Abihu, the sons of Aaron, took either of them his censer, and put fire therein, and put incense thereon.

And offered strange fire before the LORD, which he commanded them not.

And there went out fire from the LORD, and devoured them, and they died before the LORD.

Then Moses said unto Aaron, This is it that the LORD spake, saying, I will be sanctified in them that come nigh me, and before all the people I will be glorified.

And Aaron held his peace.

**Narrator**

This is one of the most sobering moments in Leviticus.

Leviticus 9 ended with fire accepting the offering.

Leviticus 10 begins with fire judging unauthorized worship.

Nadab and Abihu offer strange fire.

The text explains what made it strange.

God had not commanded it.

That is the issue.

They approach holy service by human invention.

They bring worship God did not authorize.

And fire comes out from before the LORD.

This time it does not consume the offering.

It consumes the priests.

Moses says God will be sanctified in those who come near Him.

That means the people closest to holy service must treat God as holy.

Aaron holds his peace.

His silence is heavy.

The chapter does not make this easy.

It makes it serious.

God's nearness is beautiful.

But it is never casual.

## Leviticus 10:8-20

Leviticus 10 verses 8 through 20.

**Scripture**

And the LORD spake unto Aaron, saying,

Do not drink wine nor strong drink, thou, nor thy sons with thee, when ye go into the tabernacle of the congregation, lest ye die.

And that ye may put difference between holy and unholy, and between unclean and clean.

And that ye may teach the children of Israel all the statutes which the LORD hath spoken unto them by the hand of Moses.

And Moses diligently sought the goat of the sin offering, and, behold, it was burnt.

And when Moses heard that, he was content.

**Narrator**

After the death of Nadab and Abihu, the LORD speaks directly to Aaron.

The priests must serve with clear judgment.

They must not enter holy service under the influence of wine or strong drink.

Why?

Because priests must put difference between holy and unholy.

Between unclean and clean.

They must teach Israel the statutes of the LORD.

That is a major priestly calling.

Priests are not only people who handle sacrifices.

They are teachers of holy distinction.

They help Israel understand what belongs near God's presence and what does not.

The chapter ends with Moses and Aaron working through how the sin offering should be handled after such a painful day.

Moses listens.

And when he hears Aaron's answer, he is content.

Even in grief, holy service must be handled carefully.

## Leviticus 11:1-23

Leviticus 11 verses 1 through 23.

**Scripture**

And the LORD spake unto Moses and to Aaron, saying unto them,

Speak unto the children of Israel, saying, These are the beasts which ye shall eat among all the beasts that are on the earth.

Whatsoever parteth the hoof, and is clovenfooted, and cheweth the cud, among the beasts, that shall ye eat.

Nevertheless these shall ye not eat of them that chew the cud, or of them that divide the hoof.

These shall ye eat of all that are in the waters: whatsoever hath fins and scales in the waters, in the seas, and in the rivers, them shall ye eat.

And all that have not fins and scales in the seas, and in the rivers, they shall be an abomination unto you.

**Narrator**

Leviticus 11 begins the clean and unclean food laws.

To modern readers, these laws can feel strange.

But they are not random.

They train Israel to make distinctions.

Land animals must chew the cud and have split hooves.

Water creatures must have fins and scales.

Certain birds and winged creatures are forbidden.

Some locust-like insects are allowed.

At every meal, Israel is reminded that they belong to the LORD.

Holiness is not only something that happens at the altar.

It reaches the table.

It reaches ordinary choices.

It forms a people who learn to ask, what has God called clean?

And what has God called unclean?

## Leviticus 11:24-47

Leviticus 11 verses 24 through 47.

**Scripture**

And for these ye shall be unclean: whosoever toucheth the carcase of them shall be unclean until the even.

And upon whatsoever any of them, when they are dead, doth fall, it shall be unclean.

And every earthen vessel, whereinto any of them falleth, whatsoever is in it shall be unclean.

For I am the LORD your God: ye shall therefore sanctify yourselves, and ye shall be holy; for I am holy.

For I am the LORD that bringeth you up out of the land of Egypt, to be your God.

Ye shall therefore be holy, for I am holy.

**Narrator**

The second half of Leviticus 11 explains uncleanness through contact with dead animals.

Death matters in Leviticus.

Decay matters.

Contact with carcasses can make people, clothing, vessels, ovens, and food unclean.

Unclean does not always mean morally sinful.

That is important.

Sometimes uncleanness marks contact with death, disorder, or what does not belong near holy space.

But the chapter ends with the reason behind all of this.

Be holy, for I am holy.

God rescued Israel from Egypt to belong to Him.

So their lives must be shaped by His holiness.

The food laws are not just diet rules.

They are identity training.

Israel is learning to be a set-apart people.

## Leviticus 12:1-8

Leviticus 12 verses 1 through 8.

**Scripture**

And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,

Speak unto the children of Israel, saying, If a woman have conceived seed, and born a man child: then she shall be unclean seven days.

And in the eighth day the flesh of his foreskin shall be circumcised.

And when the days of her purifying are fulfilled, for a son, or for a daughter, she shall bring a lamb of the first year for a burnt offering, and a young pigeon, or a turtledove, for a sin offering.

And if she be not able to bring a lamb, then she shall bring two turtles, or two young pigeons.

And the priest shall make an atonement for her, and she shall be clean.

**Narrator**

Leviticus 12 gives instructions after childbirth.

This does not mean childbirth is sinful.

It does not mean babies are bad.

The chapter is dealing with ritual impurity connected to blood, birth, and returning to sanctuary worship.

The child is circumcised on the eighth day.

That connects the child to the covenant sign God gave Abraham.

After the purification period, the mother brings offerings.

And again we see God's mercy for the poor.

If she cannot bring a lamb, she may bring two birds.

That detail will matter later in the Bible.

In Luke 2, Mary brings the poor person's offering after Jesus is born.

Leviticus is not just ancient background.

It helps us understand the world Jesus entered.

## Closing

Day 34 teaches that God's nearness is beautiful and serious.

In Leviticus 9, fire accepts the offering.

In Leviticus 10, fire judges strange fire.

The same holy God who welcomes worship His way also warns against treating Him casually.

Then Leviticus 11 and 12 show that holiness reaches ordinary life.

Food.

Bodies.

Birth.

Waiting.

Washing.

Family life.

God is forming a people who can live near Him.

Tomorrow, Leviticus turns to uncleanness, restoration, and one of the most important days in Israel's worship life:

The Day of Atonement.
