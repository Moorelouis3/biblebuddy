# Day 97 Narrator Script

## Opening

Welcome to Day 97 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading First Chronicles 13 through 16.

The title for today is The Ark Comes to Jerusalem.

This day centers on David bringing the ark of God to Jerusalem.

At first, the joy is real but the handling is wrong.

Uzza dies when he touches the ark.

David becomes afraid and leaves the ark at the house of Obed-edom.

Then David learns the proper order.

The Levites carry the ark as God commanded.

The singers, musicians, priests, and people rejoice.

The ark is brought into the tent David prepared, and David appoints worship before the LORD.

Day 97 teaches that zeal for God must be joined to obedience to God's word.

## First Chronicles 13

First Chronicles 13 begins with David consulting leaders and calling Israel to bring the ark again.

The desire is good.

The ark had not been properly sought in Saul's days.

The people agree, and the assembly rejoices.

But they carry the ark of God on a new cart.

That is the problem.

God had given instructions for the ark to be carried by the Levites.

When the oxen stumble, Uzza puts out his hand to hold the ark.

The anger of the LORD breaks out against him, and he dies there before God.

David is displeased and afraid.

He asks how he shall bring the ark of God home to him.

The ark is taken to the house of Obed-edom, and the LORD blesses that house.

The chapter is sobering.

Holy things cannot be handled by human convenience.

Joy does not erase the need for obedience.

## First Chronicles 14

First Chronicles 14 shows David established in Jerusalem.

Hiram king of Tyre sends messengers, timber, masons, and carpenters.

David knows the LORD has confirmed him king over Israel because his kingdom is lifted up for the sake of God's people.

That line keeps David's success in the right place.

David is established for the people, not merely for himself.

Then the Philistines come against him.

David asks God whether he should go up.

God answers, and David defeats them at Baal-perazim.

When the Philistines come again, David asks God again.

This time God gives different instructions.

David obeys and wins.

The chapter shows David learning dependence.

He does not assume yesterday's instruction is enough for today's battle.

He inquires of God.

## First Chronicles 15

First Chronicles 15 returns to the ark.

David prepares a place for it and says none ought to carry the ark of God but the Levites.

He understands what went wrong the first time.

He gathers priests and Levites and tells them to sanctify themselves.

Because they did it not at the first, the LORD made a breach upon them.

Now they seek Him after the due order.

That phrase is key.

The ark comes to Jerusalem with singers, instruments, priests blowing trumpets, and Levites carrying the ark upon their shoulders with staves, as Moses commanded according to the word of the LORD.

There is gladness, but now gladness is ordered by obedience.

David dances and plays before God.

Michal sees him and despises him in her heart.

The chapter contrasts humble worship with proud contempt.

## First Chronicles 16

First Chronicles 16 shows the ark set in the midst of the tent David pitched.

Offerings are made.

David blesses the people and gives bread, flesh, and wine.

Then he appoints Levites to minister before the ark, to record, thank, and praise the LORD.

David delivers a psalm of thanks.

The psalm calls Israel to give thanks, call on the LORD's name, make His deeds known, sing to Him, glory in His holy name, seek the LORD, and remember His marvelous works.

It remembers covenant with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

It declares that the LORD is great and greatly to be praised.

It calls the nations and creation to worship.

The chapter ends with worship ordered in Jerusalem and at the high place in Gibeon.

The people depart every man to his house.

The ark has come, and worship has been established.

## Full Lesson Review

Day 97 moves from mistaken zeal to ordered worship.

David wants the ark near, but the first attempt ignores God's command.

The second attempt follows the word.

The Levites carry the ark.

The priests and singers serve.

The people rejoice.

The psalm teaches Israel how to praise.

The lesson is clear: worship must be joyful, but it must also be holy.

## Slow Walk

Pay attention to David's growth.

He begins with consultation and excitement.

Then he is humbled by the breach against Uzza.

He learns from the word.

He corrects the process.

He gathers the right servants.

He brings the ark in the due order.

This is repentance in leadership.

David does not give up on worship after failure.

He returns to God's command.

That is the path forward.

## Deeper Teaching

Day 97 is one of the clearest lessons in Chronicles about worship.

The first attempt to bring the ark looks sincere.

David consults leaders.

The people agree.

They gather from across Israel.

They use music and celebration.

But sincerity does not make disobedience safe.

The ark is carried on a new cart, and that is not the way God commanded Israel to carry it.

When Uzza touches the ark, the breach is terrifying.

The listener should not read this as God being careless with human life.

The point is that God's holiness is real.

The ark represents the presence and covenant throne of the LORD.

It cannot be handled as if human instinct is enough.

David's fear after Uzza's death is part of his learning.

He stops.

He does not keep forcing the plan forward.

The ark rests with Obed-edom, and blessing comes to that house.

First Chronicles 14 then shows David's leadership in battle.

The Philistines attack twice.

David inquires of God twice.

God gives different instructions.

That matters because obedience is living dependence, not automatic repetition.

David is learning to ask, listen, and act under God's direction.

First Chronicles 15 shows the corrected worship.

David says none ought to carry the ark but the Levites.

He names the failure: they did it not at the first, so the LORD made a breach.

Then he says they must seek God after the due order.

That phrase is the heart of the day.

Due order means worship is shaped by God's word.

The Levites sanctify themselves.

The singers are appointed.

The instruments are arranged.

The priests blow trumpets.

The ark is carried with staves on the shoulders of the Levites.

The joy is still there, but now the joy is obedient.

First Chronicles 16 then gives the people words for worship.

Give thanks.

Call on His name.

Make known His deeds.

Sing.

Seek His face.

Remember His covenant.

Declare His glory among the nations.

The chapter teaches that worship includes memory, mission, thanksgiving, and reverence.

It is not only a feeling in the moment.

It is the ordered response of God's people to God's covenant faithfulness.

## Extended Narration

Day 97 is a powerful reading because it refuses to let worship become casual.

David wants the ark in Jerusalem, and that desire is good.

The ark belongs at the center of Israel's life because it represents the covenant presence of the LORD among His people.

But the first attempt goes wrong.

The ark is placed on a new cart.

The people celebrate.

The music is loud.

The moment feels joyful.

Then Uzza puts forth his hand to steady the ark, and the LORD smites him.

That is hard for many readers because the action can look helpful from the outside.

Chronicles wants the reader to see that holy things must be handled according to God's word, not merely according to human instinct.

Good intentions do not cancel God's instructions.

Sincere emotion does not replace obedience.

Religious excitement does not make a careless approach safe.

David becomes afraid, and the ark is left in the house of Obededom.

But the story does not end in fear.

God blesses Obededom's house.

That blessing teaches that God's holiness is not against His people.

God's presence is not poison.

The problem was not that the ark came near.

The problem was that the ark was approached wrongly.

That distinction matters for the whole Bible.

The God who is holy is also the God who blesses.

The God who judges irreverence is also the God who gives joy when His people come near in the way He commands.

Then Chronicles moves into David's battles with the Philistines.

David asks God whether he should go up.

God answers.

David obeys.

Then the Philistines come again, and David asks again.

This repeated seeking is part of the lesson.

David does not assume yesterday's answer is automatically today's strategy.

He inquires of God again.

That is very different from Saul, who failed to inquire of the LORD.

David's victories are not presented as raw military genius.

They are victories under God's direction.

When the ark finally comes to Jerusalem in chapter 15, David corrects the earlier failure.

The Levites carry the ark.

The priests and Levites sanctify themselves.

The singers are appointed.

The instruments are arranged.

The people rejoice.

This time the joy is joined to reverence.

That is the Bible's picture of true worship.

It is not dead formality.

It is not careless excitement.

It is glad obedience before the holy God.

Chapter 16 then gives the words of praise.

The song calls Israel to give thanks, call upon His name, make known His deeds, sing unto Him, remember His marvelous works, and glory in His holy name.

That is worship with memory and mission.

Israel is not only supposed to feel thankful inside.

Israel is called to speak God's works aloud.

The nations are supposed to hear.

The earth is told that the LORD reigneth.

Chronicles is showing worship that looks backward to God's covenant, upward to God's holiness, outward to the nations, and forward to His kingdom.

For a beginner, Day 97 teaches that God cares how His people come near.

The ark cannot be treated like ordinary furniture.

The name of the LORD cannot be treated like a decoration for human plans.

But when God's people come in reverence, obedience, and praise, the result is not cold fear.

The result is joy.

The result is thanksgiving.

The result is a people who can say, Give thanks unto the LORD; for he is good; for his mercy endureth for ever.

## Final Guided Recap

Day 97 begins with a holy mistake and ends with ordered praise.

The ark is desired, mishandled, feared, then finally brought in the right way.

David grows through correction.

The people learn that worship cannot be carried by good intentions alone.

The Levites must carry what God assigned them to carry.

The singers must sing.

The priests must serve.

The people must remember the LORD.

The listener should leave this day with a balanced vision of worship.

God welcomes joy.

God commands reverence.

God gives songs.

God gives order.

God is worthy of both gladness and obedience.

## Chapter Listening Guide

In chapter 13, listen for the danger of a right desire handled the wrong way.

David wants the ark near.

The people rejoice.

But the ark is not carried as God commanded.

The breach against Uzza forces David and the reader to face the holiness of God.

In chapter 14, listen for David asking God.

The Philistines attack twice.

David does not simply trust his own instincts.

He inquires.

When God gives one command for one battle and another command for the next, David obeys both.

In chapter 15, listen for correction.

David names what went wrong.

He gathers the Levites.

He prepares the singers.

He brings the ark according to the word.

This is what leadership should do after failure: learn, humble itself, and obey.

In chapter 16, listen for worship expanding into mission.

The psalm does not only say thank you privately.

It says make known His deeds among the people.

It says declare His glory among the nations.

The ark in Jerusalem becomes a center of praise that looks outward to the whole earth.

## Closing

As you finish Day 97, remember the due order.

God is worthy of joy, music, dancing, thanksgiving, and praise.

He is also worthy of obedience.
