# Bible in One Year - Day 45 Narrator Script

**Reading:** Numbers 26-29

## Opening

Welcome to Day 45 of Bible in One Year.

Today we are reading Numbers chapters 26 through 29.

The theme is a new generation counted.

The first wilderness generation has fallen.

But God's promise has not failed.

Now the LORD counts a new generation, protects inheritance, appoints Joshua, and orders Israel's worship before they enter the land.

## Numbers 26 verses 1 through 65

**Scripture**

After the plague, the LORD tells Moses and Eleazar to count the congregation.

The children of Israel are counted by tribes and families.

The count is connected to the inheritance of the land.

The chapter also counts the Levites.

It closes by saying that none of the first counted generation remains except Caleb and Joshua.

**Narrator**

This census is not just a list.

It is proof that God preserved a people.

The unbelieving generation died in the wilderness, just as the LORD warned.

But Israel did not disappear.

The promise continues through their children.

The count prepares for inheritance.

🧮 Names counted

🏕️ Tribes preserved

🌄 Land prepared

God is moving from wilderness judgment toward promised inheritance.

## Numbers 27 verses 1 through 11

**Scripture**

The daughters of Zelophehad come before Moses, Eleazar, the princes, and the congregation.

Their father died in the wilderness and had no sons.

They ask why his name should be removed from his family.

Moses brings their cause before the LORD.

The LORD says they speak right and gives a law for inheritance.

**Narrator**

This moment matters because the daughters bring a real family problem into the presence of God.

Their concern is not selfish ambition.

They want their father's name and inheritance preserved.

Moses does not guess.

He brings the case before the LORD.

God's answer protects inheritance and gives Israel a just pattern for the future.

This shows that God's law is not blind to real people.

It speaks into actual family life.

## Numbers 27 verses 12 through 23

**Scripture**

The LORD tells Moses to go up and see the land.

Moses will not enter because he rebelled at the waters of Meribah.

Moses asks God to set a man over the congregation so they will not be like sheep without a shepherd.

The LORD appoints Joshua.

Moses lays his hands on him before Eleazar and the congregation.

**Narrator**

Moses cannot enter the land.

That is painful.

But Moses' heart is still pastoral.

He asks God to care for the people after him.

The phrase sheep without a shepherd shows their need for guidance, protection, and leadership.

Joshua is appointed publicly.

The mission does not die with Moses.

God provides the next leader for the next stage.

## Numbers 28 verses 1 through 31

**Scripture**

The LORD gives commands about offerings.

There are daily offerings, Sabbath offerings, monthly offerings, Passover offerings, Unleavened Bread offerings, and offerings for the day of Firstfruits.

The offerings are called a sweet savor unto the LORD.

**Narrator**

After counting and leadership, God turns to worship.

That order matters.

The new generation does not only need land.

They need life with God.

Daily offerings teach regular devotion.

Sabbath offerings teach weekly rhythm.

Feast offerings teach holy memory.

🔥 Daily worship

📅 Holy time

🌾 Harvest gratitude

God is preparing Israel to enter the land as worshipers, not just settlers.

## Numbers 29 verses 1 through 40

**Scripture**

The LORD gives offerings for the seventh month.

The chapter includes the day of blowing trumpets, the Day of Atonement, and the feast of Tabernacles.

Many offerings are repeated day by day.

Moses tells the children of Israel all that the LORD commanded.

**Narrator**

The seventh month is full of worship.

Trumpets gather attention.

The Day of Atonement calls for humility.

Tabernacles teaches joy and remembrance.

The repetition can feel long, but it is teaching steady worship.

Israel's year is not supposed to be shaped only by farming, travel, and family plans.

It is shaped by the LORD.

The God who gives the land also orders the calendar.

## Closing

That is Day 45.

Numbers 26 through 29 shows a new generation counted and prepared.

God gives inheritance justice.

God appoints Joshua.

God orders worship.

The wilderness has been hard, but the promise is still alive.
