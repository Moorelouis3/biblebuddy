# Bible in One Year - Day 38 Narrator Script

**Reading:** Leviticus 25-27; Numbers 1

## Opening

Welcome to Day 38 of Bible in One Year.

Today we close Leviticus and begin Numbers.

We are reading Leviticus chapters 25 through 27, and Numbers chapter 1.

The theme is Jubilee, covenant, and Israel counted.

Leviticus ends by reminding us that the land, the people, the vows, the blessings, and the warnings all belong to the LORD.

Then Numbers begins by counting Israel for the journey ahead.

## Leviticus 25 verses 1 through 22

**Scripture**

The LORD speaks to Moses in Mount Sinai.

The land must keep a Sabbath unto the LORD.

For six years Israel may sow and prune, but in the seventh year the land must rest.

After seven sabbaths of years comes the fiftieth year, the Jubilee.

Liberty is proclaimed throughout the land, and every man returns to his possession and to his family.

**Narrator**

The Sabbath year teaches that the land belongs to God.

Israel may work the land, but they do not own it as if God has disappeared.

The Jubilee goes even deeper.

It protects freedom.

It protects inheritance.

It protects families from permanent loss.

Liberty is not just a nice idea here.

It is built into Israel's life because the LORD is the true owner and redeemer.

The people must learn to trust God even when obedience interrupts normal production.

## Leviticus 25 verses 23 through 55

**Scripture**

The LORD says the land shall not be sold forever, because the land is His.

Israel are strangers and sojourners with Him.

If a brother becomes poor, the people must not take advantage of him.

Relatives may redeem land.

Poor brothers must be helped.

Servants from Israel must not be ruled with rigor, because the children of Israel are the LORD's servants whom He brought out of Egypt.

**Narrator**

This section explains redemption in everyday life.

Redemption means buying back what was in danger of being lost.

A family member could redeem land.

A poor brother could be helped.

A servant could eventually go free.

The reason is repeated.

Israel belongs to the LORD.

They are not free to treat one another like Egypt treated them.

God's rescue from bondage must shape how they handle land, money, debt, power, and poor neighbors.

## Leviticus 26 verses 1 through 13

**Scripture**

The LORD commands Israel not to make idols.

They must keep His Sabbaths and reverence His sanctuary.

If they walk in His statutes and keep His commandments, He will give rain, harvest, peace, safety, victory, fruitfulness, and His presence.

The LORD says He will walk among them and be their God, and they shall be His people.

**Narrator**

This is covenant blessing.

The blessing is not only about crops and safety.

The greatest promise is God's presence.

He says He will walk among them.

That language takes us back toward Eden.

God wants His people to live with Him.

Rain, harvest, peace, and victory are real blessings.

But they are not the center.

The center is relationship with the LORD.

## Leviticus 26 verses 14 through 46

**Scripture**

The LORD warns Israel what will happen if they will not listen.

Discipline will increase if they continue in rebellion.

The land will enjoy its Sabbaths while they are in their enemies' land.

Yet if they confess their iniquity, the LORD will remember His covenant with Jacob, Isaac, Abraham, and the land.

**Narrator**

This chapter is heavy because covenant rebellion is serious.

God is not threatening randomly.

He is warning His people before they walk into ruin.

The repeated pattern is refusal to listen, then stronger discipline.

But mercy is still present.

If the people confess, God remembers His covenant.

That matters.

Israel's hope is not that they will never fail.

Their hope is that the LORD remembers His promise.

## Leviticus 27 verses 1 through 34

**Scripture**

Leviticus ends with vows, valuations, dedicated people, animals, houses, fields, devoted things, and tithes.

The chapter teaches how dedicated things may be valued or redeemed.

It closes by saying these are the commandments which the LORD commanded Moses for the children of Israel in Mount Sinai.

**Narrator**

This chapter can feel like a strange ending.

But it fits Leviticus.

It teaches that what is promised to the LORD must be treated honestly.

Vows are not games.

Dedicated things are not casual.

The tithe is holy unto the LORD.

Leviticus ends by reminding Israel that holiness reaches promises, property, animals, fields, money, and worship.

If it belongs to the LORD, it must be handled as holy.

## Numbers 1 verses 1 through 54

**Scripture**

The LORD speaks to Moses in the wilderness of Sinai, in the tabernacle of the congregation.

Israel is numbered by tribes, families, fathers' houses, and names.

The men twenty years old and upward, able to go forth to war, are counted.

The Levites are not numbered with the armies.

They are appointed over the tabernacle of testimony, its vessels, and its service.

**Narrator**

Numbers begins with a census.

That means the people are counted.

But this is not just bookkeeping.

Israel is being ordered for the journey.

The tribes are named.

The families are remembered.

The fighting men are counted.

The Levites are set apart for the tabernacle.

The people who came out of Egypt are becoming an organized camp in the wilderness.

God is preparing them to move forward with His presence at the center.

## Closing

That is Day 38.

Leviticus closes by teaching that the land, freedom, vows, blessing, warning, and holy things belong to the LORD.

Numbers opens by showing Israel counted and ordered for the wilderness journey.

The same God who rescued them is now arranging their life.

He owns the land.

He remembers His covenant.

He receives what is holy.

He counts His people.

And He prepares them to move.
